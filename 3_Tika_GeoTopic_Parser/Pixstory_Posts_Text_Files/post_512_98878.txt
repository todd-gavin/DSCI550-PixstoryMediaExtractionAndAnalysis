In India almost all urban households and rural households have access to an improved source of drinking water Improved sources of water protect against outside contamination so that the water is more likely to be safe to drink Urban and rural households rely on different sources of drinking water The main sources of drinking water for urban households are water piped into their dwelling yard or plot tube wells or boreholes and public taps or standpipes In contrast rural households rely most on tube wells or boreholes followed by water piped into their dwelling yard or plot In rural areas percent of households have water on their premises or delivered to their dwelling compared with percent in urban areas In households where water is not delivered or the source of water is not at the premises women age years and above are most likely to collect drinking water Clean water is a basic need for human life However percent of households do not treat their water prior to drinking Treatment is less common in rural areas than urban areas percent of rural households do not treat their water compared with percent of urban households Boiling water and straining the water through a cloth before drinking are the most common water treatment procedures used prior to drinking and of households respectively
