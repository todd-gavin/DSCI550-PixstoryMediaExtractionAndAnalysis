There are some great philosophical books out there that will challenge your views on the world and make you think about the things that matter most. Here are four of my picks:
1. The God Delusion by Richard Dawkins
This book is a masterwork of scientific skepticism and atheism, and it provides an in-depth analysis of religious belief. It's a highly controversial book, but it's definitely one that will change your perspective on religion and how it affects society. 
2. The Tao of Pooh by Christopher Robin
This classic children's book is full of wisdom and insights about life that can be applied to adult life as well. It explores topics like self-awareness, balance, and living in harmony with others. 
3. The Catcher in the Rye by J.D. Salinger
This novel is a must-read for anyone who wants to understand teenage angst and angst in general. Holden Caulfield reflects on his experience as a teenager during the 1950s, and he offers an insightful look at growing up in America during that time period.
