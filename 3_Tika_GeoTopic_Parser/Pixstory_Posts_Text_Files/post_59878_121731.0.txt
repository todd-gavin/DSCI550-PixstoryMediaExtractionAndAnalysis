According to Aam Aadmi Party MP Sanjay Singh, following a meeting of the party's political affairs committee today, the party will support joint Opposition candidate Margaret Alva in the upcoming elections to choose India's Vice President.

"Arvind Kejriwal, the Delhi Chief Minister and national convener of the Aam Aadmi Party, spoke over a meeting of the Political Affairs Committee. Who the Aam Aadmi Party will back in the election of the vice president was thoroughly examined. 

We will back Margaret Alva, the opposition candidate, in the election for vice president, Arvind Kejriwal and the PAC members have unanimously agreed. Our entire Rajya Sabha delegation would support them on June 6th" he said.

Ms. Alva expressed gratitude to the AAP's MPs, leaders, and Arvind Kejriwal for their support.
AAP has previously backed Yashwant Sinha, a joint opposition candidate, in the presidential race.
Former Bengal Governor Jagdeep Dhankhar has been chosen by the NDA as its candidate for the position.

The Jharkhand Mukti Morcha (JMM), which is led by Hemant Soren, declared support for Ms. Alva earlier in the day. Shiv Sena has also vowed to back Ms. Alva.
77-year-old Margaret Alva has held governorships of Goa, Gujarat, Rajasthan, and Uttarakhand. She served as a Cabinet Minister in the Rajiv Gandhi administration and was a five-time member of parliament.
