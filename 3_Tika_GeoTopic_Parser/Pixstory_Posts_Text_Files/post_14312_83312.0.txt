FLYING START - With a goal after only 32 seconds, Dušan Vlahović became the player to take the least time to score his first goal in the European Cup/Champions League for Juventus; he is also the first among the Bianconeri to score on his debut in the modern Champions League in the knockout phase.

AGE GAP - At 22 years and 25 days, Vlahović is the second youngest player to score on a Champions League debut for Juve, after Alessandro Del Piero (20 years 308 days).

FIRST UP - With the 1-1 draw in the first ever match against Villarreal, Juventus remained unbeaten in the first leg of the Champions League Round of 16 for the first time since February 2018, a 2-2 draw with Tottenham Hotspur.

HALF CENTURY - At the Estadio de la Cerámica, Juan Cuadrado made his 50th appearance in the UEFA Champions League, considering matches from the group stage to the final.
