Direct heat used to dry or style hair can cause heat damage The hair s curl pattern may irreversibly lose integrity as a result of excessive heat and or prolonged direct heat application leaving the hair limp and lifeless In more extreme situations the hair is burned split and shed In fact if you don t protect your hair from the sun direct sunlight can harm it Keep your hair damp and styled in a protective manner if you plan to spend the entire day in the sun or in the humid air When you blow dry your hair air is forced through it at varying temperatures and wind speeds The outer cuticle of the hair may get ruffled if airflow is directed from the tips of the hair up to the roots Over time frequent use of heating appliances such as blow dryers straightenes or curling iron can weaken the hair s outer layer making it brittle and prone to breaking from the bottom up
