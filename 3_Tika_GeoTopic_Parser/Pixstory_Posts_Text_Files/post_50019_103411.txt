T S Eliot was a poet essayist publisher playwright literary critic and editor He is considered one of the th century s major poets he is a central figure in English language Modernist poetry Eliot first attracted widespread spotlight for his poem The Love Song of J Alfred Prufrock in which at the time of its publication was considered outlandish The opening lines of the poem are now famous comparing the evening sky to a patient etherised upon a table were considered shocking and offensive especially at a time when Georgian Poetry was hailed for its inceptions of the nineteenth century Romantic Poets The poem waste land is known for its vague nature its slippage between irony and prophecy its abrupt differences in speaker location and time This structural complexness is one of the reasons why the poem has become a touchstone of modern literature a poetic counterpart to a novel published in the same year James Joyce s Ulysses Ash Wednesday is the first long poem written by Eliot after his transformation to Anglicanism Published in it deals with the battle that occurs when a person who has lacked faith acquires it Sometimes pertained to as Eliot s conversion poem it is richly but ambiguously allusive and deals with the aspiration to move from spiritual barrenness to hope for human salvation One cannot deny that his experiments in diction style and versification resurrected English poetry and in a series of critical essays he broke down old orthodoxies and built new ones
