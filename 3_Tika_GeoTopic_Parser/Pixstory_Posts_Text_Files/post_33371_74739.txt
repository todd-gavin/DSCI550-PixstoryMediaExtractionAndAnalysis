Visit to Narwar Fort continues The Fort of Narwar The Fortress of Narwar is situated on a bend of the Sindh River on an irregularly shaped hill upwards of feet in height just miles to the south south west of Gwalior and to the same distance to the west north west of Jhansi Shape and Size Its shape is peculiar and may be likened best to the head and neck of a duck The central portion or the head is called Majha Mahal or middle quarter and also Bala Hisar or the citadel as it commands the other divisions of the fort The neck or northern quarter is called Madar hata as it contains a shrine of the famous Muhammadan Saint Shah Madar The duck s Bill is called Dulha Kot or the bridegroom s fort because the last of the Kacchwaha princes is said to have made his escape from Narwar by a postern gate at its east end The citadel is cut off from Madar Hata by a high wall feet in length and from Dulha Kot by another wall about feet in length The circuit of the Majha Mahal is rather more than miles that of Madar Hata is nearly miles and that of dulha Kot only three quarters of a mile The whole circuit is therefore miles The English traveler Finch mentioned the circuit as or kos and Ferishta stated that the circumference is kos Principal Gates The Principal entrance lies in the hollow forming the duck s throat to the Alamgiri Darwaza From here there is a flight of steps to the Hawa Paur or Gaumukhi Gate via the Saiyidon ka Darwaza and the Piron Paur or the Pir s Gate The lower gate was not built during the reign of Aurangzeb but was renamed as it was observed by Finch in The Saiyidon ka darwaja bears a short inscription dated in AD Its original Hindu name is unknown To be continued
