After facing military setbacks, Russian President Vladimir Putin has signed the final papers to annex four regions of Ukraine. 
According to the official document, Luhansk, Donetsk, Zaporizhzhia and Kherson regions are now inscribed into the Russian Federation. 
Putin has also signed a decree to formalise Russia's seizure of the nuclear power plant in Zaporizhzhia. 
Besides this, the decree exempts students of different categories, including first-time students at accredited institutions, and certain types of postgraduate students those in the field of science. 
While Ukrainian forces are gaining in both the south and the east, Russian leader followed a self proclamatory move last week, where he signed agreements with the Moscow-installed leaders of the four regions, in Kremlin. 
Meanwhile, On Thursday, the southern city of Zaporizhzhia experienced huge explosions which makes it more clear that Russia would retake any territory that had been lost to Ukrainian forces.
