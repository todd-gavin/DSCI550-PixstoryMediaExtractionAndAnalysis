 The term is to refer to the post World War I generation Of writers The lost generation refers to the loss of traditional values and inherited spirits and disorientation that the war provided to the writers The term embraces Ernest Hemingway F Scott Fitzgerald John Dos Passos E E Cummings Archibald MacLeish Hart Crane and many other writers who made Paris the centre of their literary activities in the s They were never a literary school For example e e cummings disregarded the capitalisation of letters and grammar rules out of disillusionment out of war and universality Mainly consisted of writing on the themes of world war and its aftereffects One of the themes that commonly occurs in the authors works is decadence and the frivolous lifestyle of the wealthy Like Nick Carraway in Great Gatsby who realizes the corruption around him in many novels the death of the American dream is exhibited throughout many of their novels
