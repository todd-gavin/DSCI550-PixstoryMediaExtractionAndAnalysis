With appropriate treatment many people successfully address the significant symptoms of dissociative identity disorder and improve their ability to function and live productive fulfilling life Treatment typically involves psychotherapy Therapy can help people gain control over the dissociative process and symptoms The goal of therapy is to help integrate the different elements of identity Therapy may be intense and complex as it involves remembering and coping with past traumatic experiences Cognitive behavioural therapy and dialectical behavioural therapy are two commonly used types of therapy Hypnosis has also been helpful in the treatment of dissociative identity disorder There are no medications to treat the symptoms of dissociative identity disorder directly However medication may help treat related conditions or symptoms such as the use of antidepressants to treat symptoms of depression
