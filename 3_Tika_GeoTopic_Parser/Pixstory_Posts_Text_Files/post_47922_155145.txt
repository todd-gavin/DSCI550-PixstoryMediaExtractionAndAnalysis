By NewsDrum desk New Delhi The Enforcement Directorate has filed a charge sheet against journalist Rana Ayyub under the anti money laundering law alleging that she utilised publicly raised funds of Rs crore for herself and also contravened the foreign contribution law The federal agency filed its prosecution complaint against Ayyub before a special Prevention of Money Laundering Act PMLA court in Ghaziabad Uttar Pradesh on October Rana Ayyub launched three fundraiser charity campaigns on Ketto platform starting from April and collected funds totalling Rs the ED said in a statement issued on Thursday The campaigns it said were meant to raise funds for slum dwellers and farmers relief work for Assam Bihar and Maharashtra and help Ayyub and her team to help those impacted by COVID in India Probe found the agency said that the funds raised on the online platforms were received in the accounts of Ayyub s father and sister and subsequently transferred to her personal accounts Ayyub utilised these funds to create a fixed deposits of Rs lakh for herself and also transferred Rs lakh in a new bank account Investigation found that only about Rs lakh was used for relief work it claimed In order to claim more expenses towards relief work fake bills were submitted by Ayyub and subsequently bank balances in the accounts of Ayyub amounting to Rs including FD of Rs Lakh were attached under the PMLA vide a provisional attachment order dated it said The ED alleged Ayyub illegally raised the Rs crore funds and cheated general public donors These funds were not used for the intended purpose and instead used for creation of assets for herself Ayyub has tried to project these funds as untainted and thus has laundered the funds received from general public the agency alleged Ayyub also received these funds from foreign countries without any approval or registration from the government which is required under the Foreign Contribution Regulation Act it said To read more
