According to a research released on Tuesday, an increasing number of individuals are choosing to ignore major news items including the coronavirus epidemic, Russia's invasion of Ukraine, and the cost-of-living crises. 
The majority of those polled said they routinely consume news, while 38 percent said they frequently or occasionally avoid it, up from 29 percent in 2017, according to the Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism's annual Digital News Report. 36 percent of people, especially those under 35, claim that the news makes them feel down.
