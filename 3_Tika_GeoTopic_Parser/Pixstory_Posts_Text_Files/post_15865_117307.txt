The effect of weeks aerobic resistance and combined trainings on peripheral vascular disease in type diabetes with peripheral neuropathy in men Introduction Type diabetes is caused by more complications One complication that occurs in some cases is peripheral vascular disease Early diagnosis is very important to assess peripheral vascular disease Material and methods men with type diabetes were randomly divided into four groups of aerobic resistance combined and control Intensity of aerobic exercise was percent of maximum heart rate for min Resistance training consists of two to three sets with repetitions and combined training consists of both aerobic and resistance training three times a week for weeks Ankle brachial index ABI measurements and blood samples were analyzed before and after intervention The collected data were analyzed using ANOVA Results The resistance and combined training groups showed a significant increase in the left ABI compared to pretest P Conclusions The resistance training group was more effective than aerobic and combined training program in reduction the peripheral vascular disease Sources
