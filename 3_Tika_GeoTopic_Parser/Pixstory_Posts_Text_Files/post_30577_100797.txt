The tenth stage of the Giro d Italia km from Pescara to Jesi ended with the first success of an Eritrean rider in the history of the pink race. Biniam Girmay was obviously the protagonist, already second on the finish line of the first stage held in Hungary. This time the year-old winner of Ghent Wevelgen succeeded to beat Mathieu van der Poel who had preceded him on that occasion while Vincenzo Albanese placed third Previously the stage had lived on the escape attempted by Bas De Marchi and Naesen caught up to km from the finish For the winner Girmay also a curious mishap while celebrating success on the podium the cork of the sparkling wine hit him in the eye forcing him to undergo checks at the hospital in Jesi Tomorrow another flat stage dedicated to sprinters
