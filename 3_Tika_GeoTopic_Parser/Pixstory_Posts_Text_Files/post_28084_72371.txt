In recent days, the Government&#39;s choice to limit the capacity of stadiums to seats in response to the exponential increase in Covid infections after the Christmas holidays has caused discussion. A measure that has lent itself to criticism due to some smaller stadiums that are remained almost full and others such as the San Siro in Milan and the Olimpico in Rome which, on the contrary, were almost deserted despite the large spaces to contain the public, guaranteeing a discreet distance. Returning to the topic today was the former Italian athlete Valentina Vezzali, now undersecretary for sport. in a couple of weeks the situation will be better, then it will be possible to start again from the outdoor facilities and from the arenas with the hope of reaching full capacity when normality returns
