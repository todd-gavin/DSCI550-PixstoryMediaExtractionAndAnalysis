Tourists have returned to one of the most famous selfie spots in New York if not the world the Brooklyn waterfront in Dumbo where the arch of the Manhattan Bridge frames the Empire State Building in the distance The people who live there are not too happy writes Ginia Bellafante in the Big City column of the New York Times In essence, she tells of a place that used to be a quiet glimpse of the city transformed into a high-impact tourist attraction from selfies with the addition of street vendors and sandwich makers stationed under the windows of the residents Not a good life for who has a house there And who knows how many places in the rest of the world suffer from the same problem Chefchauen the blue city of Morocco comes to mind for example
