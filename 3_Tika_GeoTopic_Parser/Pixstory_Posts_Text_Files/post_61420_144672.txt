Gratin dauphinois Gratin dauphinois is a classic French dish consisting of sliced potatoes cream of milk butter cheese and herbs cooked together The potatoes are cooked using the gratin technique The dish originated in the Dauphin region in the south east of France and is said to have been invented in the 18th century It is also called dauphinoise potatoes potatoes la dauphinoise and potato gratin la dauphinoise Peeled potatoes the size of a pi These coins are layered in a glass baking dish and baked in a slow oven The best side dishes to serve with potato gratin are kale and Brussels sprout salad or roasted mushrooms
