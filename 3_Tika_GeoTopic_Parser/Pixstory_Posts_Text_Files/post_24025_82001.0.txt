Thousands of years in the past – 240 million to be precise – a spine-chilling archosaur roamed Tanzania. What made it menacing were its extremely “powerful jaws and large knife-like teeth”, according to a recent study cited in livescience.com.

It was not just the jaws and teeth that were intimidating as the creature of the Triassic period, measured from snout to tail more than 16 feet or five metres and has been christened Mambawakale ruhuhu meaning “ancient crocodile from the Ruhuhu Basin" in Kiswahili.
