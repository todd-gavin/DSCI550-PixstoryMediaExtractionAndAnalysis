In the Gender Gap Report 2022 from the World Economic Forum, India came in at number 135 out of 146 nations. On a scale of 0 to 1, the nation received a score of 0.629, which ranks as its seventh-highest mark in the previous 16 years.

India has the biggest improvement in its performance in terms of economic opportunity and participation.

The nation, however, has a falling political empowerment score and the lowest position on the subindex for health and survival.

India has the worst survival and health index with more than 5 percent gap in between gender.

In four crucial areas—political empowerment, health and survival, educational attainment, and economic involvement and opportunity—the Global Gender Gap Index measures how far the globe has come toward gender parity.
