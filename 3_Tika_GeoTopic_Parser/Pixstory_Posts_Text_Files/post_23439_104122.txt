Croatia should become the 20th member of the euro area on January 1 The country born from the disintegration of Yugoslavia over the years obtained the green light from the European Commission today Wednesday June The final approval from the Council should come in July after an assessment by the Bank Central Committee of Finance Ministers of the Eurogroup and the European Parliament In its report, the Community executive notes that Croatia complies with the four convergence criteria set out in the treaties and which concern the trend of interest rates, inflation of public finances and the exchange rate Furthermore, Brussels explains that the Croatian legislation is fully compatible with the European Treaties
