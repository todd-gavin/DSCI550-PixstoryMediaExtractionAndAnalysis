The Snake Goddess ceramic statuette dated to C was found fragmented in by the archaeologist Evans in the underground treasury chamber of the central sanctuary of the palace of Knossos Island of Crete The particularity of this statuette is its incredible strength an element that has always been considered traced back to man Among the her characteristics are the breasts and the hips particularly highlighted this is because the mother goddess is the one who gives life thanks to her fertility The Greeks identify the deity with Potnia theron or lady of the animals with this term some female deities associated with animals were indicated on which they were able to exercise power The Goddess of Serpents teaches us that there are no male or female attributes if you are strong you are strong period Source Giulia Civita
