An ode is a lyric poem elaborate in structured praising an event or individual describing nature intellectually as well as emotionally The three typical forms of odes are the Pindaric Horatian and irregular Pindaric odes follow the form and style of Pindar Horatian odes follow the conventions of Horace the odes of Horace deliberately imitated the Greek lyricists such as Alcaeus and Anacreon Irregular odes use rhyme but not the three part form of the Pindaric ode nor the two or four line stanza of the Horatian ode The ode is a lyric poem It conveys heightened and inspired emotions It is a lyric in an elaborate form conveyed in a language that is imaginative dignified and honest Like the lyric an ode is of Greek origin
