By NewsDrum Desk New Delhi Bajaj Auto on Monday said it has commenced its Rs share buyback programme In a regulatory filing the Pune based company said the buyback initiative has begun with effect from July The company s board at its meeting held on June had approved the proposal for buyback of the fully paid up equity shares of the company with a face value of Rs each from existing shareholders except promoters and promoter group from the open market The buyback will be carried out at a price not exceeding Rs per equity share and for an aggregate amount of up to Rs crore representing per cent of the aggregate of the total paid up share capital of the company it had stated Shares of the company were trading per cent up at Rs apiece on the BSE
