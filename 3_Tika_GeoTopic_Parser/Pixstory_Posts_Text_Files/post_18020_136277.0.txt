The makers of hit Netflix series “The Crown” have revealed who is set to play Prince William and Kate Middleton in Season 6.
Two actors have been cast as the young prince: 16-year-old Rufus Kampa and 21-year-old Ed McVey. Meanwhile Meg Bellamy will play Kate Middleton.
It will feature an all-new cast including Imelda Staunton as Queen Elizabeth, Jonathan Pryce as Prince Philip, Lesley Manville as Princess Margaret, and Olivia Williams as Camilla Parker Bowles.
Prince Harry will be played by Teddy Hawley and Will Powell in the fifth season of "Harry and Meghan," according to producers of the BBC drama. It will also feature a young Prince William, played by Senan West, and Prince Harry at different ages. The casting suggests that Season 5 will focus on the 1990s, including events leading up to and following Diana's death.
Downton Abbey Season 6 is reportedly set to focus on the late 1990s and early 2000s. Prince William was 15 in 1997, when his mother, Diana, died. He took a gap year before enrolling at St Andrew's University in 2001, where he met Kate Middleton. They went on to date for 10 years before eventually tying the knot in 2011.
Her former butler Paul Burrell was accused of stealing hundreds of items belonging to the princess. The case was halted at the last minute after it emerged she had given him permission to take them.
Kampa, McVey and Bellamy will all make their professional screen debuts this season. Emma Corrin became a household name after playing Princess Diana in Season 4 while Josh O'Connor played Prince Charles in Seasons 3 and 4. The series changes up its cast every two seasons to portray the royals in different decades.
