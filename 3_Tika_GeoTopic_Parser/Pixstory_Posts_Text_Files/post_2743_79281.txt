As Vice President in Joe Biden launched the Cancer Moonshot a mission to speed up the fight against cancer On Wednesday he reignited the mission setting a new goal to cut cancer death rate by at least per cent over the next years and improve quality of life of those living with and surviving cancer At an election campaign rally in Iowa in Biden had vowed to cure cancer if he is elected as president in Improving cancer care has been his long time focus as he lost his year old son Beau to brain cancer in Some medical experts had raised questions about his lofty goal and termed it aspirational because there are over types of cancer and a single cure for them all is most unlikely Now he renewed his pledge to fight the disease but in a more pragmatic way and the word cure was not used in the agenda He reminded that cancer is one of the reasons he ran for president and it remains his priority since it s still the number two cause of death in America In the last two years of the pandemic cancer claimed lives of million Americans far more than deaths due to Covid The president urged healthcare administrators to get cancer screenings back on track and make sure they re accessible as nearly million cases of cancer were missed in the past two years of Covid The renewed moonshot will build a cancer cabinet to coordinate between federal departments various agencies and experts He urged Congress to fund the new Advanced Research Project Agency for Health ARPAH a new autonomous entity within NIH and appealed to private drug companies to make more affordable drugs However no fresh funding commitments were announced Despite the president s ambitious goals some critics argue that prevention is still the best way to tackle cancer initiatives to reduce smoking and rates of obesity are sure shot methods to cut cancer death rate READ MORE
