The dying of 30-year-old Indian-origin Mandeep Kaur in New York, who was tortured at the hands of her husband, has sparked rage across India and abroad, putting forward concerns yet again over domestic violence. 

The Indian Embassy in New York on Saturday conveyed grief over Kaur's death in Queens and said it was in touch with the US administrations at all levels and will induce all kinds of assistance needed.

Embassy Tweeted, "We are deeply saddened by the death of Mandeep Kaur in Queens, New York under most tragic circumstances. We are in touch with the US authorities at Federal and local level as well as the community. We stand ready to render all assistance."

Mandeep Kaur moved to New York after her marriage but was allegedly physically abused day in and day out by her husband Ranjotveer Singh Sandhu reportedly for not bearing a son, as well as over dowry. She ended her life on August 3. The suicide has sent shockwaves.
