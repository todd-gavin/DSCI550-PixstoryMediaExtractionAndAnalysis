This beautiful lil bird is known by the name Orange headed Ground Thrush Zoothera citrina a bird of the thrush family that is commonly found in well wooded areas of India China and Southeast Asia Most populations of the species are resident and are often spotted in the hills of southern India and the Western Ghats The species shows a preference for shady damp areas and like many Zoothera thrushes can be quite secretive In their natural habitat these ground thrushes nest in trees and are an omnivorous species The orange headed thrush is a shy secretive bird usually occurring alone or in pairs but is comparatively more easily seen than many other Zoothera thrushes and several birds may congregate outside the breeding season at a good food source It has a swift silent flight but when disturbed will often sit motionless until the threat has passed Such an adorable creature
