Using the Artemis Real time Orbit Website AROW you can follow NASA s Orion spacecraft as it completes its first orbit of the Moon As per NASA TV Orion will conduct the first combined flight test with the Space Launch System SLS rocket during Artemis I travelling miles beyond the Moon Almost anyone with internet access can use AROW to locate Orion and monitor its location distance from the Earth and Moon mission duration and other information Starting on August AROW will be accessible via the NASA website and the NASA Orion Twitter account AROW displays information gathered by Orion s sensors and transmitted to the mission control facility at NASA s Johnson Space Center in Houston throughout the spacecraft s voyage Periodically it will deliver real time data This is a really powerful way to engage with the mission and understand the scope of what NASA is trying to accomplish with Artemis I said Seth Lambert the Orion programmer who created AROW
