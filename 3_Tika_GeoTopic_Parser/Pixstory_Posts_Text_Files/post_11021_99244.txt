Bengaluru In Jan an independent body of experts created to evaluate projects by their environmental harm or benefit rejected a proposal from the forest department to recognise Hesaraghatta a lush grassland and lake on Bangalore s northwestern edge as a conservation reserve If it was recognised as a conservation reserve Hesarghatta would have legally been considered a protected area alongside national parks and sanctuaries Instead only two of the members of the State Board for Wildlife SBWL supported the proposal which was rejected according to the minutes of the meeting held on January This was the only decision that the current board has taken in nearly months of its existence No infrastructure project can be cleared in a forest area without clearance from the SBWL in a state where millions of trees over an area of about sq km were lost over years to a decline that is steadily accelerating at a time of global warming Hesaraghatta is an ecologically important grassland and lake that hosts endangered bird species such as the Lesser Florican which is protected under schedule I the highest level of protection offered by the Wild Life Protection Act WLPA Hesarghatta was also an important source of water for the city until the mid s before its catchment area was encroached or built on Environmental experts had hoped that once it became a protected area the ecosystem could be saved and the catchment area revived to support the growing water needs of a city of more than million people Karnataka s SBWL was meant to be a guardian of sorts for such protected areas In India s federal system the SBWL is an important part of the environmental regulatory apparatus at the state level said M D Madhusudan former member of the National Board for Wildlife NBWL Read more
