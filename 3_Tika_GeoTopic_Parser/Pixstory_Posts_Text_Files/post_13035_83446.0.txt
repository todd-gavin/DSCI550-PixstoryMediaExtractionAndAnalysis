The Royal Society of British Television presented this year's Special Award to Afghan Journalists. This prestigious and historical association commended Afghan journalists for their contribution to covering Afghanistan in the world media.

The association wrote on its official Twitter page that "this year's award is dedicated to Afghan journalists, who over the past fourteen years have been relied on by all major media outlets in the world to cover and report on Afghanistan."

The Royal Society of British Television was founded in 1927 as a charitable organization training in the field of television.

The association says it has 3,500 active members and is supported by reputable international media outlets such as the BBC, Sky News and Channel 24.
