With Hall of Famers Dave DeBusscher and Bill Bradley at forward, Willis Reed at centre, and Walt Frazier at guard, the 1969–70 Knicks won the NBA title.

DickBarnett was the other starting guard. Cazzie Russell and Dave Stahlworth were among the reserves. The Knicks also won the NBA championship in 1972–73.

The starting lineup has the same four hall of famers, as well as hall of famer Earl The Pearl Monroe at the opposite guard spot. In the starting lineup, there are five Hall of Famers. Jerry Lucas (who filled in for Reed on several occasions) and Dick Barnett were also on the roster at forward/center positions.

With six Hall of Fame players on the roster, the 1972–73 club wins the award for best Knicks team.
