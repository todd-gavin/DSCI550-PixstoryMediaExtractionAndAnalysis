Fans going to the World Cup in Qatar must show a negative COVID test when they arrive as part of the host nation s rules to combat COVID organisers said Thursday All visitors aged and over must also download a government run phone application tracking people s movements and health status called Ehteraz A green Ehteraz showing the user does not have a confirmed case of COVID is required to enter any public closed indoor spaces World Cup organizers said Visitors must be able to show a negative result from a PCR test taken in the hours before arriving or from an official rapid test taken within hours The COVID testing policy for visitors aged six and over is regardless of the individual s vaccination status the Supreme Committee for Delivery amp Legacy said in a statement Vaccination not necessary Vaccination is not mandatory for the million expected visitors for the November December tournament Rapid antigen tests taken in the previous hours before landing in Qatar will only be accepted if they are from official medical centers and not self administered No further tests are required in Qatar if fans do not develop symptoms of COVID The decisions extend throughout the World Cup public health policies in place since Sept for all travellers arriving in Qatar Mask compulsory Masks must be worn on public transport including the subway system that many fans will use to get to the eight stadiums in and around Doha Qatar has recorded nearly confirmed cases of COVID and deaths from the disease according to data gathered since by Johns Hopkins University in the United States Qatar has a population estimated to be at least million although only about of those are Qatari citizens More than of the population in Qatar has had at least one dose of a COVID vaccine the data states Anyone who tests positive for COVID while in Qatar will be required to isolate in accordance with Ministry of Public Health guidelines World Cup organizers said
