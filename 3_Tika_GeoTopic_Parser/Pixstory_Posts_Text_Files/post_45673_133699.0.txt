The greatest test flight of the year for NASA is about to take place. 

Space.com reported that the countdown to NASA's Artemis 1 mission, an ambitious first trip to the moon using the agency's most potent rocket — the Space Launch System (SLS) — and its Orion spacecraft, began today, August 27, at 10:23 a.m. EDT (1423 GMT). The unmanned test flight will lift off from Pad 39B at the Kennedy Space Center on Monday, August 29, at 8:33 a.m. EDT (12:33 a.m. GMT). 

According to Jim Free, NASA's assistant administrator for exploration systems development, "this initial launch is another step in the blueprint of our sustainable exploration of the solar system. View the Artemis 1 moon mission  launch live online, courtesy of NASA TV. A live webcast will begin Monday at 6:30 a.m. EDT (1030 GMT).
