Muslims throughout the world are celebrating Eid al Fitr which marks the conclusion of Ramadan the holy month in which believers fast from sunrise until nightfall The Islamic calendar s Eid al Fitr or Festival of Breaking the Fast occurs on the first day of Shawwal The start of any lunar Hijri month changes depending on when the new moon is sighted by local religious authorities therefore it does not always fall on the same day Eid al Fitr usually abbreviated as Eid is a three day Muslim feast that can be longer or shorter depending on the country It s a time to celebrate with friends and family while also offering appreciation to God Celebrations begin with a special early morning prayer in mosques and open air spaces and later move on to feasts and festivals
