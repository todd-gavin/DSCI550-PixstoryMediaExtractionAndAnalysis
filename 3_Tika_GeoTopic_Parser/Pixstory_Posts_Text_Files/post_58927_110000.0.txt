Votes are currently being counted for three Lok Sabha seats—Azamgarh, Rampur, and Sangrur in Uttar Pradesh; Mandar, in Jharkhand; Atmakur, in Andhra; and Agartala, Town Bordowali, Surma, and Jabarajnagar, in Tripura—as well as seven Assembly seats.

With a margin of about 11,000 votes, the Aam Aadmi Party won the Rajinder Nagar byelections in Delhi on Sunday. Manik Saha, the Tripura chief minister, defeated Ashish Kumar Saha, the candidate for the Congress, by 6,104 votes in the Town Bardowali bypolls. To keep his position as CM, Manik Saha needs to win this election. Mekapati Vikram Reddy of the YSR Congress Party defeated the BJP candidate in Atmakur, Andhra Pradesh, by a significant majority of 82,888 votes.

While the Samajwadi Party had been in the lead in the opening round of tallying in Uttar Pradesh, the BJP has now surpassed it with 11484 and 1814 votes, respectively, in Rampur and Azamgarh.

Simranjit Singh Mann of the SAD (Amritsar) has now overtaken Gurmail Singh of the AAP as counting continues after a close race in Sangrur, Punjab. After sweeping to victory in the assembly elections, the ruling Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) is now facing its first popularity test.
