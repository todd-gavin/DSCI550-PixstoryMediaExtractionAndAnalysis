NASA will launch its powerful rocket for an earlier-than-expected orbit around the moon. 

Space.com reported that the Artemis 1 Space Launch System (SLS) rocket was supposed to make the long journey to Launch Complex 39B at Kennedy Space Center on Thursday, August 18, in preparation for its launch on August 29. 

However, NASA announced (opens in new tab) on Monday (Aug. 15) that the rollout had been moved up to Tuesday evening (Aug. 16). NASA will be providing coverage of the rollout starting at 3 p.m. EDT (1900 GMT).

"@NASA is targeting as soon as 9 pm EDT of Tuesday, Aug. 16 for rollout of NASA_SLS ahead of a targeted Aug. 29 #Artemis I launch," agency officials wrote in a tweet.
