Australia’s Ashleigh Barty, on Wednesday, March 23, announced his retirement from professional tennis at the age of 25. The Queensland-born tennis star bowed out on a high being the World No.1 and the second player from his country after Evonne Goolagong Cawley to hold the top ranking. Barty has held the ranking for as long as 114 weeks. The youngster also achieved a career-high ranking of No.5 in doubles.  

Having won 15 singles and 12 doubles titles, Barty was counted amongst the very best on the court. Her first major breakthrough came when she defeated Czech Republic’s Marketa Vondrousova 6-1, 6-3 in the 2019 French Open. Barty, the then No.7 seed, thereby became the first Australian to win at the Roland Garros since 1973. 

She won her second title after beating Karolina Pliskova 6-3, 6-7, 6-3 in the 2021 Wimbledon at the All-England Club in London. Earlier this year, Barty beat Danielle Collins 6-3, 7-6 to win the Australian Open at the Rod Laver Arena in Melbourne. In the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, she won the bronze medal in the women’s doubles after partnering Storm Sanders. 

Ashleigh Barty Net Worth 

Barty currently has a net worth of USD 23.8 million. After becoming the No.1 tennis player following her 2019 triumph at Rolland Garros, Barty started earning sponsorship and endorsements. In 2020, she was the third-highest paid female athlete in the world, Forbes reported. As per reports, Barty had earned USD 10.1 in prize money and a total of USD 13.1 million. 

Last, she was the highest-paid women’s tennis player after earning approximately USD 1.104 million in prize money from 32 games. Her net worth hovered around the $20 million mark after she penned down delas with various companies. 

Back in 2018, she struck a deal with automobile giant, Jaguar after which she was signed as one of the ambassadors for Kayo Sports, a video streaming platform. She is also endorsed by Australian food spread firm, Vegemite. Barty has also been associated with Gillette Venus, Uber Eats and Rado.
