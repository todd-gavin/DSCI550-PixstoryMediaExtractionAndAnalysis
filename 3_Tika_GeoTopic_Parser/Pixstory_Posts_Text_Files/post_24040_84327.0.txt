As a long march to oust  the Pakistani Prime Minister Imran Khan kicked off on Sunday from Karachi to Islamabad, the opposition leaders have asked the military establishment to stay neutral when they bring the no-confidence motion against the Imran Khan government.  The co-chairman of the opposition, Pakistan People’s Party (PPP), Bilalwal Bhutto claimed that the combined opposition has support of 200 parliamentarians which is enough to “kick out” Imran Khan and his government through a no-confidence vote. According to the Pakistani constitution, opposition parties need only 172 votes out of the 342 seats of the National Assembly.
