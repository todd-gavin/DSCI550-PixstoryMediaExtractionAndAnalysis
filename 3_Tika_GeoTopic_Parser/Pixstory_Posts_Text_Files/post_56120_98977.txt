The masque was a form of festive courtly entertainment that prospered in th and early th century Europe though it was developed earlier in Italy in forms including the intermedio A masque involved music and dancing singing and acting within a vast stage design in which the architectural framing and costumes might be constructed by a renowned architect to present a deferential allegory flattering to the patron Professional actors and musicians were hired for the speaking and singing parts William Shakespeare included a masque like interlude in The Tempest understood by modern scholars to have been heavily impacted by the masques of Ben Jonson and the stagecraft of Inigo Jones The most outstanding humanists poets and artists of the day in the full emphasis of their creative powers dedicated themselves to producing masques and until the Puritans closed the English theatres in the masque was the highest art form in England
