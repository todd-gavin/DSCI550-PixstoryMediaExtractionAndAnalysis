An epigraph refers to a phrase quotation or poem that is set at the beginning of a book The epigraph may serve as a preface to the work as a summary as a counter example or as a link from the work to a wider literary canon For example The long quotation from Dante s Inferno that prefaces T S Eliot s The Love Song of J Alfred Prufrock is part of a speech by one of the damned in Dante s Hell The epigraph is a very complicated form of literary device that can brush up a story very well The use of epigraph in an original work can create something very intriguing It can be used as a thematic gatekeeper by taking excerpts from influential authors to introduce people to your ideas It can be used in the form of quotations proverbs lyrics lines verses or even parts of a conversation It can also be used to set the mood of the readers in the very beginning for the prose they are to read next
