Muslims view Muharram as having tremendous significance since under the Islamic calendar it is the first month of the year The new Islamic year begins this time on July Al Hijri or the Arabic New Year is another name for the first day of Muharram according to the lunar calendar that Islam uses Ashura or the tenth day is observed by the Muslim community worldwide Imam Hussain the grandson of the Prophet Muhammad and the son of Hazrat Ali was a martyr and Shia Muslims all over the globe mourn his passing The days leading up to Ashura are marked by community wide marches in mourning Additionally it is asserted that on the day of Ashura Allah delivered Prophet Musa Moses and the Israelites from the army of Pharaoh Firaun Many Shia Muslims take part in these processions and some even intentionally harm themselves to mimic Imam Hussain s anguish
