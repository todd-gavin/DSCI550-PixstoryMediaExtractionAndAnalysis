We studied pieces of litter to find out where it all comes from here s what we discovered Litter is perhaps the most tangible of all environmental problems And it s not just a disrespectful few who are responsible for it Litter defined in its broadest terms includes any solid material present in the environment that was made or processed by people It may have arrived there from an accidental spillage as debris washed ashore or because of the irresponsible management of industrial waste How some types of litter enter and travel through the environment cannot be traced Litter breaks down beyond recognition identifying marks such as branding wear away and rivers can transport it far from where it originated But some litter has clear sources and pathways discernible from its function and packaging labels from which the brand that made the item can be easily identified The community interest company Planet Patrol created an app for people to record the litter they find and remove We used it to map the location materials type and where possible brands of items of litter collected across the UK in Our research was recently published in the Journal of Hazardous Materials Plastic was the most common material recorded accounting for of all items Metal was second followed by composite materials pieces of litter made from more than one material like Tetrapak cartons at Bottles lids straws and other items from the drinks industry made up of the total of which metal cans were the most common Our citizen scientists identified brands for items of the total with of these belonging to just ten brands The Coca Cola Company was the most frequently identified brand of branded litter followed by Anheuser Busch InBev and PepsiCo The top three brands were all drinks manufacturers Read more
