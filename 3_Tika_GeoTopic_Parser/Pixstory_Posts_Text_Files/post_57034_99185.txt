Literature has always find its ways to portray the beauty of women in every form And what else would be more wonderful than motherhood Many writers have showed their creativity to showcase motherhood in their novels They are rich in narrative opportunities offering a journey full of conflict joy struggle and pain both physical and emotional Yet they are rarely the subject of fiction Female characters have babies yes but that is so often the end of their story it s much neater and more pleasingly metaphorical to end a novel with a pregnancy The Handmaid s Tale by Margaret Atwood Beloved by Toni Morrison Anna Karenina by Leo Tolstoy We Need to Talk About Kevin by Lionel Shriver Me and the Fat Man by Julie Myerson Night Waking by Sarah Moss
