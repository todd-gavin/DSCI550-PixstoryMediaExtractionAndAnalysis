Varanasi also called Banaras or Kashi is southeastern Uttar Pradesh state northern India Located on the left bank of the Ganga River and is one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism History Varanasi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world It was one of the first major urban settlements in the middle Ganges valley By the nd millennium BCE Varanasi was a seat of Vedic religion and philosophy and was also a commercial and industrial centre famous for its muslin and silk fabrics perfumes ivory works and sculpture It was the capital of the kingdom of Kashi during the time of the Buddha Varanasi remained a centre of religious educational and artistic activities In the British made Varanasi a new Indian state with Ramnagar as headquarters but with no jurisdiction over the city of Varanasi In after Indian independence the Varanasi state became part of the state of Uttar Pradesh The contemporary city Varanasi has the finest river frontage in India with miles of ghats for religious bathing an array of shrines temples and palaces rises tier on tier from the water s edge The inner streets of the city are narrow winding and impassable for motor traffic The sacred city is bounded by a road known as Panchakosi devout Hindus hope to walk that road and visit the city once in a lifetime and if possible to die there in old age In addition thousands of domestic and foreign tourists flock to the city annually and tourism related activities constitute a significant component of the local economy There are innumerable schools and countless Brahman pandits who are responsible for the continuation of traditional learning There are three universities and more than a dozen colleges and high schools The city is centre of arts crafts music and dance Varanasi is famous for its production of silks and brocades with gold and silver threadwork A renowned carpet weaving centre is Bhadoi
