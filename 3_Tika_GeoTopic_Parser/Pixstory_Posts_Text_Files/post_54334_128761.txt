The Confederation for African Football Caf recently announced plans for a continent wide Africa Super League It will kick off with clubs from countries in August The new tournament will run annually from August until May with games in a format much like the European UEFA Champions League It starts off with the teams grouped in three zones North Africa West Central Africa and East Southern Africa The top clubs move into a knockout phase The enticement is a projected US million windfall from Caf with of the funds going to the development of women s and youth football The rest goes into prize money for participating clubs The champion club will receive US million and all members of Caf will get US million each This is much bigger than the US million received by current winners of the African Champions League club tournament The African Champions League and the Confederation Cup will continue with entries from all African countries but the format for both competitions will return to the earlier iteration of two legged elimination contests For Caf which lost an estimated US million in the league is a way to earn big money from television rights However Caf provided very few answers to questions about the practical realities of the league when its plans were announced in Tanzania on August There are pertinent questions that need clarifying but even at this early stage it s clear that there are strengths and weaknesses to the shiny new Africa Super League Issues to be clarified There are at least two major issues that Caf should clarify The proposed Super League which runs for the entire football season involves clubs that will at the same time be playing in their national club competitions Continental competitions often disrupt local leagues In places like the Democratic Republic of Congo and Nigeria clubs sometimes travel by road three times the distance clubs in England travel for league games Read more
