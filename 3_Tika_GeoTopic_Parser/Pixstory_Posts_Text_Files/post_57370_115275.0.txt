Eggs
Eggs are high in protein and several essential nutrients. As a result, they may promote fullness and help lower calorie intake later in the day.

Greek yoghurt
Greek yoghurt is not only high in protein and low in calories, but certain types are also high in probiotics, which support gut health.

Coffee
The caffeine in coffee promotes alertness and enhances physical and mental performance. In addition, drinking coffee regularly is associated with a lower risk of illnesses.

Oatmeal
Oatmeal is rich in beta-glucan, a type of fibre that may lower cholesterol levels and increase feelings of fullness. It also contains many vitamins and minerals.

Chia seeds
Chia seeds are high in fibre, which may help promote fullness and reduce hunger levels.

Berries
Berries are high in fibre and low in calories. They’re also rich in antioxidants that may decrease your risk of disease.

Cottage cheese
Cottage cheese is high in protein, which may help keep you full and reduce hunger. Everyday breakfast toppings include fresh fruit, veggies, seeds, and granola.

Whole wheat toast
Whole wheat toast is a good source of fibre. Plus, you can top it with a wide variety of nutritious spreads.

Nuts
Nuts are a filling, nutrient-dense food that may help reduce heart disease risk and promote brain health.

Green tea
Green tea contains caffeine and an antioxidant called EGCG, which is tied to a reduced risk of chronic disease and better brain health.

Protein shakes
It’s easy to whip up a protein shake or smoothie in a few minutes and take it on the go. Plus, this simple breakfast makes a great post-workout option.

Fruit 
Eating various fruits provides you with different vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. What’s more, most fruits contain high amounts of fibre that may promote fullness and stabilise your blood sugar levels.
