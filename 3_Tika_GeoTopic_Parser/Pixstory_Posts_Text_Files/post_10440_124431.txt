The visits to the thousand-year-old Novalesa Abbey, immersed in the greenery of Val Cenischia, continue also in August. The Benedictine abbey dedicated to Saints Peter and Andrew was founded in to the community of Benedictine monks A place of worship and prayer but also a treasure trove of art, it offers a unique cultural heritage that the Metropolitan City of Turin makes available to visitors free of charge in respect of the balance between opening to the public and the times of monastic life Below are the opening hours in force in August to visit the Abbey with its frescoed chapels and the Archaeological Museum
