The number of new COVID-19 infections in India increased by 20,408 in a single day, bringing the country's total to 4,40,0138, while the number of active cases decreased to 1,43,384, according to the Union Health Ministry on Saturday, July 30, 2022. The number of fatalities has increased by 44, bringing the total to 5,26,312.

The national COVID-19 recovery rate was reported at 98.48%, whereas the active cases make up 0.33 percent of all infections.

In the last 24 hours, there has been a 604 case reduction in the active COVID-19 caseload.

A 5.05 percent daily positive rate was noted. The health ministry said that the weekly positive rate was 4.92 percent.

A record 4,33,30,442 persons made a full recovery from the virus, and the case fatality rate was 1.20 percent.

The government reports that 203.94 crore doses of the COVID vaccine have been been given throughout the nation as part of the COVID-19 vaccination drive.

On August 7, 2020, India's COVID-19 total had surpassed the 20-lakh barrier, and on December 19, it had surpassed the one-crore level.

On May 4, 2021, June 23, 2022, and January 25, this year, India reached the terrible milestone of two crore, three crore, and four crore, respectively.

44 new fatalities were reported, including six each from Maharashtra and West Bengal, four from Haryana and Karnataka, three from Gujarat, Punjab, and Uttar Pradesh, two from each of Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Jammu and Kashmir, and Madhya Pradesh, as well as one each from Chandigarh, Delhi, Goa, Himachal Pradesh, Kerala, Odisha, and Sikkim.

5,26,312 deaths have been recorded nationwide so far, with 1,48,097 of those occurring in Maharashtra, 70,451 in Kerala, 40,143 in Karnataka, 38,032 in Tamil Nadu, 26,308 in Delhi, 23,565 in Uttar Pradesh, and 21,352 in West Bengal.
