In a once quiet local Salafi group called Boko Haram became increasingly violent in north east Nigeria and border communities of Niger Chad and Cameroon Its quest to forbid western education in the heavily Islamic region has led to the kidnapping of many school children the slaughter of an estimated teachers and the burning down of schools But the terrorist group was largely unknown on the world stage until the kidnapping of schoolgirls from the Nigerian town of Chibok in April The hashtag BringBackOurGirls used in the international calls for the girls to be rescued garnered over million tweets in less than a month It became Twitter s most used hashtag at the time reaching million tweets in It inspired protests around the world demanding the immediate release of the girls Some of these girls are still in captivity today others have been rescued or have escaped But some of the rescued girls returned to their abductors It was a sign that there was more to know about Boko Haram terrorism In a recent study I argue that the global popularity of the Chibok girls case is due to the hard work of the BringBackOurGirls campaign Its framing of the case however failed to offer a nuanced picture of realities in Nigeria The violence of Boko Haram in the north east is much more complex than the gendered divisions female victims versus male perpetrators that emerged I turned to the testimonial stories that emerged after the kidnapping to offer more insights Creative responsesWriters journalists and artists responded strongly to the BringBackOurGirls campaign The kidnapping has been taken up in novels Buried Beneath the Baobab Tree films Nollywood s Boko Haram and a scene in Hollywood s Black Panther documentaries Stolen Daughters and Daughters of Chibok poems The Book of Chibok literary memoirs A Gift From Darkness literary reportage The Chibok Girls and Beneath the Tamarind Tree visual art and songs to mention just a few Read more
