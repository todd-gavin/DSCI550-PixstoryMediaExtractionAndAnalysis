On Thursday, India recorded 7,240 new Coronavirus cases, up nearly 40% from the day before, as infections skyrocketed in numerous states, including Maharashtra.

After 94 days, India's daily coronavirus infections surpassed 5,000, bringing the total number of active cases to 32,498, according to the Union Health Ministry. According to the authorities, there are 3,641 current instances of the viral illness in the country.

According to government figures updated at 8 a.m., the number of deaths has risen to 5,24,723 with eight more fatalities. Since the beginning of the pandemic, India has reported 4.31 crore Covid cases.

Maharastra recorded 2,701 new cases, the most since January 25. At least 42% of the infections were reported to have originated in Mumbai. One B.A.5 variant case has been identified in the western state.

In the previous 24 hours, there have been 2,271 new cases registered in Kerala. In just one week, the southern state reported 10, 805 new infections.

On August 7, 2020, India's COVID-19 tally passed the 20-lakh barrier, followed by 30 lakh on August 23, 40 lakh on September 5, and 50 lakh on September 16.

On September 28, it topped 60 lakh, then 70 lakh on October 11, 80 lakh on October 29, 90 lakh on November 20, and one crore on December 19.
