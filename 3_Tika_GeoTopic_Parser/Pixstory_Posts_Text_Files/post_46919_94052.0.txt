She was one of the leading forwards in Soccer. She helped the U.S. Women’s National Team (USWNT) win two Olympic gold medals (2004 and 2012) and a World Cup. She currently stands as the highest all-time goal scorer for the national team and is second in international goals for both female and male soccer players with 184 goals, behind  Canadian Christine Sinclair. 

 She grew up playing soccer with boys as many girls were not into soccer. She broke the stereotype inspiring many girls into sports, especially soccer. She was named U.S. Soccer’s Female Athlete of the Year in 2003, a feat she achieved again in 2004, 2007, 2010, 2011, and 2013. She was included on the 2015 Time 100 list as one of the most influential people in the world.

One of her most significant goals was in the 122nd minute of the 2011 FIFA Women's World Cup quarterfinal match against Brazil. Wambach scored the equalizer in stoppage time enabling the Americans to eventually progress to the championship final against Japan after defeating Brazil in penalty kicks. Her last-minute goal set a new record for the latest goal ever scored in a match and was awarded ESPN's 2011 ESPY Award for Best Play of the Year.

She is best known for her ability to score goals back to back and is the most talented player in international competitions in soccer history.
