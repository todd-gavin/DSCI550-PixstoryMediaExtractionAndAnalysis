People find unique ways to help others in crisis and this must rank as the one of the most innovative ways. While Ukraine is under attack from Russia, Airbnb hosts are being flooded with bookings from people all over the world. Of course, they have no plans to visit Ukraine. But this is one way of sending money to the people who are in desperate need of help.

According to Airbnb, on March 2 and March 3, people from the world booked over 60,000 nights in Ukraine. (https://edition.cnn.com/travel/article/ukraine-airbnb-donations-cec/index.html) Many are crediting an Instagram influencer who first called on people to donate to Ukrainians by booking Airbnb rentals in Ukraine. Tommy Marcus, runs the Quentin Quarantino page on social media. (https://fortune.com/2022/03/04/airbnb-rentals-help-ukraine-funnel-money-ukrainians-russia-invasion/)

Ever since that appeal, Airbnb has said that over 357,000 visitors have visited the site to donate or become a host for refugees. 

One of the people supporting the Ukranians in this way is Harriet Turner of Auckland, New Zealand.  The Aucklander has booked two nights in Vovchansk, a town near Kharkiv, the city which has been hit by constant Russian shelling.  Turner says that she booked it to offer both financial and emotional support to Ukranians. (https://www.stuff.co.nz/travel/news/127971774/sending-love-and-strength-from-new-zealand-how-people-are-using-airbnb-to-help-ukraine)

Looking at how popular this got and also the publicity that Airbnb is getting , the The accommodation platform has said that it waiving all of its guest and host fees for Ukraine bookings. In other words, all the money goes to the hosts in Ukraine.

Airbnb had earlier announced soon after the breaking out of hostilities that it will offer free, temporary housing for up to 100,000 Ukrainian refugees by partnering with resettlement agencies in different parts the world. (https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2022/feb/28/airbnb-to-offer-free-housing-to-100000-ukrainian-refugees)
