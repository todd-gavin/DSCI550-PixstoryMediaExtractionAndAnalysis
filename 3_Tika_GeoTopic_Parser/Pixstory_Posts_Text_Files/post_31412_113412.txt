The Treaty on the European Union known as the Maastricht Treaty introduced the institution of citizenship of the Union Prerequisite for citizenship of the Union is citizenship of a maro State Citizenship of the Union completes national citizenship and does not replace citizens of the Union intact in addition to being able taking legal action before the EU justice bodies can take action against the State of which he is a citizen to enforce the rights due to him by virtue of his EU citizenship But the most important aspect of the discipline in question is the attribution to the EU citizen of the right to vote and to stand as a candidate in municipal elections in the Member State in which he resides as well as in elections to the European Parliament in the Member State in which he resides The Union undertakes to respect the fundamental rights as enshrined in the European Convention for the Protection of Human Rights citizen and which result from the constitutional traditions common to the member states as general principles of Community law
