Reworking a classic can be a gamble But putting the G Harrington Jacket by Baracuta in the hands of Junya Watanabe is certainly worth it The Japanese designer known for his geometric shapes and the conceptual pragmatism of his collections has re-interpreted the mythical jacket of the English brand It does so by expanding the fit and designing a patchwork on the sleeves But the real gem inside the outerwear is the addition of a removable military-inspired liner to make it reversible and suitable for even the most sizzling temperatures The rest of the design remains unchanged compared to to the primitive model with the unmistakable key elements such as the collar with double buttons, the front pockets, the check lining and the umbrella opening on the back
