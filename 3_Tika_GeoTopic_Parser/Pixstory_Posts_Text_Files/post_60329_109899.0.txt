The late Pop King Millions of followers were stunned and heartbroken when Michael Jackson passed away on June 25, 2009, at the age of 50. At the height of his success, Michael Jackson was without a doubt the biggest star in the world, even though his lavish lifestyle and legal troubles overshadowed his skills.

With Motown Records, the Jackson 5 put out 13 albums between 1969 and 1975, including singles like "ABC" and "I Want You Back."

Michael soon began making records independently of the band. Off the Wall, his first solo album for Epic, sold more than 20 million copies and was the best-selling record of 1979. Hit songs from the album included "Don't Stop 'til You Get Enough" and "Rock with You." For the Best R&B Male Vocal Performance in 1980, Michael received his first Grammy Award. Michael released the album Thriller three years later, and it topped the charts for 37 weeks. The top 10 list had seven singles, including "Beat It," "Billie Jean," and "Thriller." Michael first introduced the moonwalk, his go-to dance move, in this record. 

Eight Grammy Awards and nearly 40 million copies of Thriller were received.
Michael and Lionel Richie wrote the song "We Are The World" together in 1985. Seven million copies of the song, which included renowned performers from the era like Ray Charles, Stevie Wonder, and Cyndi Lauper, were sold. The sales revenue went toward reducing hunger in Africa.
The album "Bad" was then released in 1987 as a result of this .
