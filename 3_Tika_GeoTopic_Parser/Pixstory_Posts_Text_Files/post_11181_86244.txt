Physical fitness has been related to a lower risk of Alzheimer s disease AD but new research suggests that even moderate levels of cardiorespiratory fitness provide some protection In a news release study investigator Edward Zamrini MD of the Washington VA Medical Center in Washington DC said One intriguing result of our study is that as people s fitness increased their risk of Alzheimer s disease dropped it was not an all or nothing scenario The findings will be presented at the American Academy of Neurology AAN Annual Meeting in April Between and the researchers identified veterans mean age years who were free of Alzheimer s disease and related disorders ADRD when they performed standardized exercise treadmill tests using the Veterans Health Administration database Based on peak metabolic equivalents METs achieved during the treadmill test they categorized participants into five age specific fitness groups lowest fit METs low fit METs moderate fit METs fit METs and highest fit METs Veterans with the lowest cardiorespiratory fitness acquired ADRD at a rate of cases per person years in an unadjusted analysis compared to cases per person years in the most fit group P The findings show a inverse independent and graded relationship between cardiorespiratory fitness and ADRD risk according to the researchers conference abstract The thought that you can reduce your risk of Alzheimer s disease by just increasing your exercise is really hopeful Zamrini said in the news release particularly because there are no adequate medications to prevent or stop the progression of the disease We intend to establish a simple personalized scale so that people can recognize the benefits that even little gains in fitness may provide he said More info at
