Highways Minister Nitin Gadkari announced that a kilometer stretch of the Amravati Akola highway in Maharashtra was laid in hours and minutes breaking the Guinness World Record for the longest continuously laid bituminous lane of kilometres in hours and minutes on the national highway between Amravati and Akola districts in Maharashtra Nitin Gadkari Minister of Road Transport and Highways stated on Wednesday that the project was carried out by people including a team of independent consultants who worked around the clock The kilometres of single lane continuous bituminous concrete road is equivalent to kilometres of two lane paved shoulder road and the work began on June at a m and ended on June at p m according to a video message from the minister The previous Guinness World Record for the longest continuously laid bituminous road was set in February in Doha Qatar for constructing kilometres of road in ten days The segment between Amravati and Akola is part of NH an important east west route that connects major towns such as Kolkata Raipur Nagpur and Surat This length according to Mr Gadkari would play a significant role in alleviating traffic and freight movement on this route once completed He also complimented all of the engineers contractors consultants and workers from NHAI and Raj Path Infracon Pvt Ltd on the project s excellent execution which aided in the achievement of this world record
