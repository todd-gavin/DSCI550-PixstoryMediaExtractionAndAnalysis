If you happen to pass through the Castelli Romani area, you must visit the gardens of Ninfa. They close on November 1st and reopen in spring. It is a journey along the remains of the ancient city of which there are still the remains of houses, walls, surveillance towers and churches transformed entirely into a natural botanical garden The river creates a particular microclimate and there are centenary trees from all over the world At the moment the park is also involved in a university project that aims to raise public awareness of the importance of water and how much of it is wasted uselessly
