One of the most essential skills employers look for in employees is their ability to communicate thoughts and ideas with clarity and objective. 

In a recent survey by Bloomberg, communication emerged as a 'highly desirable but less available' skill among students along with creative thinking and risk-taking. 

In recent times, we have completely forgotten the relevance of oral communication. But oral communication has significant pertinence in the modern workplace.

Oral communication is a process in which one individual verbally interacts with another and a transfer of information takes place. However, in oral communication, you need to follow certain etiquette in order to have a healthy conversation and convey your message.
