Abbas Karimi, a swimmer of Afghan-American descent, won a gold medal in the 50-meter butterfly swimming competition in the United States.

The competition was held in Indianapolis, USA, and was the first competition for Abbas Karimi as an American citizen.

He wrote on his Facebook page that he is very excited because he is going to compete in the 50-meter backstroke tomorrow.

Backstroke is a type of swimming in which the body is placed horizontally to the back parallel to the surface of the water and the head is up to the ears in the water.

Abbas Karimi, a swimmer with a disability, has participated in several world competitions and won gold several times. Abbas Karimi is cited as a role model for a successful disabled person in Afghanistan.
