Hampi, once a part of the Vijaynagar Empire in 14th century, is a town in the state of Karnataka in India.  It is located near river Tungabhadra. UNESCO has recognized this town as a World Heritage Site. 
    This town is famous for its beautifully carved temples. 
     On the northern bank is the first capital of the Vijaynagar Empire, Anegundi.
      There are many tourist attractions in Hampi. Some of them are : 
        - Virupaksha Temple 
        - Vithala Temple 
        -  Lotus Palace 
        -  Yantrodharaka Hanuman Temple 
        -  Hampi Bazaar and many more
