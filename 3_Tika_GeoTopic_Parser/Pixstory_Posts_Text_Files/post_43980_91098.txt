The Nigerian internationals have been honoured for their outstanding performance in the previous year At the Nigeria Pitch Awards Napoli striker Victor Osimhen was named King of the Pitch while Barcelona player Asisat Oshoala was named Queen of the Pitch After the Super Eagles failed to qualify for the Fifa World Cup in Qatar the duo was honoured at a ceremony on Tuesday night After scoring goals in league matches for Barcelona in the Primera Division Oshoala beat out Gift Monday and Uchenna Kalu Since joining Blaugranes in January she has won her third consecutive league title in Spain s top division Osimhen on the other hand has had a strong season in Serie A despite injuries The year old has goals in league games this season and he was the Super Eagles leading scorer with four goals in their dismal World Cup qualification campaign Osimhen was also named Striker of the Year while Wilfred Ndidi William Troost Ekong and Maduka Okoye were named Midfielder Defender and Goalkeeper of the Year respectively Asisat Oshoala the reigning African Women s Player of the Year will compete for the Queen of the Pitch title alongside Gift Monday and Uchenna Kalu Osimhen who will miss the Africa Cup of Nations owing to injury and a positive Covid test was also mentioned in the Striker of the Year category alongside Leicester City s Kelechi Iheanacho and Onuachu who both played a key role in the club s Belgian Cup triumph in the season Oshoala has heavy competition in River Angels Gift Monday and Linkopings FC s Uchenna Kalu for the title of Nigeria s Queen of the Pitch thanks to her remarkable performance for Barcelona a squad she helped win the Uefa Women s Champions League last season
