The bitcoin price fell below $35,000 after Russian President Vladimir Putin launched a military operation in Ukraine. It has been on the slide ever since the crisis began.

With the Biden administration and many other world powers like the UK, Germany and France openly saying that they will impose sanctions on Russia and also possibly remove Russia from SWIFT (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication), often called the Gmail of global banking services. (https://edition.cnn.com/2022/02/25/business/swift-russia-putin/index.html)

Can cryptocurrencies come to the rescue of Russia to overcome sanctions is the question that is being asked around the world. Experts say that digital currencies like bitcoin and Ethereum make it easy for individuals and even governments to benefit in times of difficult times. “Neither dictators nor human rights activists will encounter any censor on the bitcoin network,” Matthew Sigel of investment manager VanEck told Bloomberg. (https://nypost.com/2022/02/24/how-russia-can-use-crypto-to-avoid-us-sanctions-over-ukraine/)

Since cryptocurrencies mask their users, they are preferred ways of money laundering for criminals and others to circumvent the traditional banking routes. In this case, if Russia faces sanctions, it could take the route of cryptocurrencies for transactions. According to experts, Iran and North Korea, which are reeling under sanctions, are using digital currencies.

What Russia may have to adapt to is something that Ukraine has mastered. It is considered the top user of cryptocurrency in Europe and the fourth biggest in the world. If it succeeds in taking complete control over the country, it could learn a few tricks. (https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2022/02/25/cryptocurrency-ukraine-russia-war-impact/)

According to some estimates, 17 million Russians own around $23 billion worth of cryptocurrency. And Russia has also been accused of being the hub of ransomware attacks through which hundreds of millions have been collected through cryptocurrency. So it wouldn’t come as a surprise if Russia employs these digital currencies, say experts. (https://nypost.com/2022/02/24/hundreds-of-russians-protest-ukraine-invasion-despite-warning-theyll-be-held-liable/)
