Venetian ladies employed a beauty tonic derived from the leaves and berries of the poisonous nightshade to redden the hue of their skin for a blush like effect throughout the Middle Ages The tonic was also used to dilate the pupils of women This was considered a stylish look and practise at the time The deadly nightshade earned its formal name Atropa Belladonna which means Beautiful Lady in Italian as a result of its appeal as a cosmetic It can be used as a pain reliever muscle relaxant anti inflammatory neuralgias whooping cough scarlet fever spasmodic asthma intestinal cramps and in eyedrops to dilate the pupil of the eyes and other medical uses Belladonna is so deadly that even a small amount of its leaves or berries can kill humans especially children as well as some animals Your skin may become irritated simply by touching the leaves
