Ali Asad, a Pakistani wrestler who won bronze for his nation in the 57kg division at the Birmingham Commonwealth Games in 2022, had his medal taken away after testing positive for a prohibited performance-enhancing substance.
In the men's freestyle 57kg wrestling competition at the Commonwealth Games 2022, Ali defeated New Zealander Suraj Singh in 55 seconds to take home the bronze medal for Pakistan, which had already won two silvers and three bronzes in the division.
Before leaving Pakistan for the CWG, the athlete had a test done to check for the presence of prohibited performance-enhancing drugs; the results were positive, it was revealed Thursday.
