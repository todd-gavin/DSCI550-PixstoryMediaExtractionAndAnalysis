Those tricks included selling the stadium to an investment fund, it was a scheme to designed to pay for further development. The right to the club badge and name were also sold.


In 2004, despite the issuing and sale of new shares, the club declared a loss of 70 million in a short time of 6 months and a debt of 120 million. The club president would resign and would be replaced by Hans Joachim Watzke who still holds his position. His short term role was to construct and sell a recovery package to more than 60 creditors and then appointing Jurgen Kloop as head coach who would help rebuild the teams identity and help them reach another Bundesliga and a UEFA cup final
