Abdurrahman Pharmacy Yusuf Murtala Bello Abubakar Abstract Background The presence of diabetes mellitus DM among COVID patients is associated with increased hospitalization morbidity and mortality Evidence has shown that hyperglycemia potentiates SARS CoV severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus infection and plays a central role in severe COVID and diabetes comorbidity In this review we explore the therapeutic potentials of herbal medications and natural products in the management of COVID and DM comorbidity and the challenges associated with the preexisting or concurrent use of these substances Methods Research papers that were published from January to December were retrieved from PubMed ScienceDirect and Google Scholar databases Papers reporting clinical evidence of antidiabetic activities and any available evidence of the anti COVID potential of ten selected natural products were retrieved and analyzed for discussion in this review Results A total of papers clinical trials on the antidiabetic activities of the selected natural products and research and review articles on their anti COVID potential were retrieved from the literature search for further analysis A total of articles reviews and less relevant research papers were excluded A cumulative sum of thirty one research papers clinical trials and others met the criteria and have been discussed in this review Conclusion The findings of this review suggest that phenolic compounds are the most promising phytochemicals in the management of COVID and DM comorbidity Curcumin and propolis have shown substantial evidence against COVID and DM in humans and are thus considered the best potential therapeutic options Sources
