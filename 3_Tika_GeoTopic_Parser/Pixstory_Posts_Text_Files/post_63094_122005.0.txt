On 16 November 2013, the Government of India selected Chintamani Nagisa Ramachandra Rao for Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian award in India, making him the third scientist after C.V. Raman and A. P. J. Abdul Kalam to receive the award. He received the award on 4 February 2014 from President Pranab Mukherjee at the Rashtrapati Bhavan.

He is described as a scientist who had won all possible awards in his field except the Nobel Prize.

Chintamani Nagisa Ramachandra Rao FNA, FASc, FRS, FTWAS, HonFRSC, MAE, HonFInstP (born 30 June 1934), is an Indian chemist who has worked mainly in solid-state and structural chemistry. He has honorary doctorates from 84 universities around the world and has authored around 1,774 research publications and 54 books.
