On this day in 1929, the Lateran Treaty came into effect. Lateran Treaty was signed between Italian fascist dictator Benito Mussolini on behalf of Italian government and Pietro Gasparri for the papacy. The treaty was important as it led to a mutual recognition of state of Italy and sovereignty of Papacy on the Vatican. The Pope became independent. Several other religious changes were made in Italy, such as end of compulsory religious schooling in Italy. 
The treaty lasted till June 1985.
