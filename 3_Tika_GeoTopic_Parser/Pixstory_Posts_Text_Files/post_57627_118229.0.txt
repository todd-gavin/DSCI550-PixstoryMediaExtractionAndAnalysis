People have different views on reading; it can help them in their ways.
But it is not just for building vocabulary, reading has many other benefits, and that's why it is one of the favourite hobbies of people.

Reading strengthens your brain. 

ResearchTrusted Source has shown that people who read literary fiction — stories that explore the inner lives of characters — show a heightened ability to understand the feelings and beliefs of others.

When you read a great story, all your stress seems to slip away and relax you since you tend to travel mentally in a different realm. Sometimes while reading, you come across valuable advice or solutions to your problems.

Regular reading is an excellent exercise for your brain. For example, if you read a fictional book, your brain is forced to remember the names and nature of various characters. Returning to their history and remembering events or plots is fun and improves memory.

One of the most notable benefits of reading every day is that it can improve your analytical skills. In addition, reading mystery novels helps you develop skills that can assist you in problem-solving.

Reading can bring back the lost power of focus and concentration. If you feel that using modern technology lowers our productivity, develop the healthy habit of reading daily.

Keeping yourself occupied with reading for about 20 to 30 minutes can be great for your brain. It can help you with better focus and concentration. However, you may have been doing it wrong for those who still find it challenging to develop focus or concentrate properly.
