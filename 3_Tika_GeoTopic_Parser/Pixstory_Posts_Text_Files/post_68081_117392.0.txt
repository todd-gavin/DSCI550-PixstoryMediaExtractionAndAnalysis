Abu Nechim Ahmed born on 5 November 1988 is an Indian cricketer. Abu has recently set up his own cricket academy namely “ANA Cricket Academy” in Guwahati,Assam. It is to be noted that he is the first Assamese international cricketer to set up his own academy in his home town. Abu Nachim, the first Assamese cricketer to participate in Indian Premier League (IPL) for seven seasons. Firstly he played for Mumbai Indians with Sachin Tendulkar for consecutive four years. Then he sign up with Royal Challenges Bangalore for three years. Presently Abu Nachim Ahmed is Playing for Nagaland in domestic cricket He is a right-arm fast bowler and right-handed lower order batsman. Earlier Abu led Assam cricket team in the Ranji Trophy, Vijay Hazare Trophy and Syed Mushtaq Ali Trophy.
Abu made his first-class debut on 1 December 2005 against Madhya Pradesh at Indore in the 2005–06 Ranji Trophy. From 2010 to 2013 he represented Mumbai Indians in the Indian Premier League. He has represented India at the U-19 level many times, played in the 2006 U-19 Cricket World Cup in Sri Lanka. He had also represented ICL India XI & Royal Bengal Tigers in the now defunct ICL.
He was the joint-leading wicket-taker for Assam in the 2017–18 Ranji Trophy, with twelve dismissals in four matches.
