 The United States is coming to an end says Stephen Marche a novelist and columnist at the start of his much talked about new book The Next Civil War Dispatches from the American Future The title and the central idea echoes another new work by political scientist Barbara F Walter How Civil Wars Start which is also hogging op ed comments these days Is the nation so polarized and on the verge of a breakdown Or is it just the liberal intelligentsia s exaggerated fears Marche explains Interview Book review Running battles between protestors and militias armed rebels attempting to kidnap sitting governors uncertainty about the peaceful transition of power reading about them in another country you would think a civil war had already begun writes the former Esquire columnist As a Canadian he argues he has an outsider s perspective to notice what s so obvious that Americans might be missing it Reviewers describe it as a work of speculative non fiction There s reporting mixed with imagined future scenarios a series of entirely possible disasters each has the US sinking in anarchy a future president is assassinated a bomb destroys Capitol white supremacist militia in gunfight with US army Marche s diagnosis The nation has become irreversibly divided and no leader in foreseeable future will be able to bring the two sides together A sense of shared purpose is missing and the institutions are failing He doesn t predict but rather considers a possibility that the US could disintegrate into four countries roughly the Northeast the West Coast the Midwest plus the Southeast and Texas Most reviewers are sympathetic but one commentator disagrees Ross Douthat the conservative columnist at the New York Times finds the civil war fantasies rather far fetched hinging on too many ifs He admits there s deep division in opinions but argues that it s limited only to beliefs and has not translated mostly into actions Also he questions Marche s definition of a civil war which
