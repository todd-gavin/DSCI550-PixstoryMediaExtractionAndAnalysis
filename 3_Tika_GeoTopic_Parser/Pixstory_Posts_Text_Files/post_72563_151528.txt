India s first interplanetary mission Mangalyaan may have come to an end when its Mars Orbiter ran out of fuel and its battery died The Rs crore Mars Orbiter Mission was launched on November using the PSLV C rocket and the MOM spacecraft was successfully sent into orbit around Mars for the first time on September Right now there is no fuel left The satellite battery has drained sources in the Indian Space Research Organisation ISRO told PTI The link has been lost However there was no official announcement from ISRO the space agency of India Fueled up ISRO had already been manoeuvring the MOM spacecraft into a new orbit to escape an approaching eclipse But recently there were back to back eclipses including one that lasted seven and half hours officials said on condition of anonymity noting that all the propellant on board the ageing satellite had been consumed
