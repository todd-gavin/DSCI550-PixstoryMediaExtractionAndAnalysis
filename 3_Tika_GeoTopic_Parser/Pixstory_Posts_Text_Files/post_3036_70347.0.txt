The World Health Organization director-general, Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus, will continue for the second term from May. He was elected unopposed in October. A former health and foreign minister from Ethiopia, Tedros received endorsement from various countries of the world. (https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2021/10/29/tedros-adhanom-ghebreyesus-set-serve-second-term-as-who-chief)

But the country of his origin is now up in arms against him. In fact, Ethiopia has asked the UN health agency to investigate Tedros for "harmful misinformation" and "misconduct", accusing him of backing Tigrayan rebels.

This is after Tedros tweeted on Jan 14 saying, “People in #Tigray #Ethiopia, living under de facto blockade for over a year, are dying from lack of medicine & food, & repeated drone attacks. WHO & partners call for safe, unimpeded access to deliver humanitarian aid to the millions of people in great need. We need access now!” (Sic)

A native of the Tigray region of Ethiopia that is in conflict with the government, Tedros has expressed his concern at the conflict which has resulted in thousands of deaths. Ethiopian government had opposed Tedros’s candidature for the second time. (https://english.ahram.org.eg/News/455254.aspx)

Ethiopia is openly accusing Tedros of siding with the Tigrayan rebels. In the latest instance, Ethiopia has accused Tedros of “interfering in the internal affairs of Ethiopia, including Ethiopia's relations with the State of Eritrea.” A letter by the Ethiopian foreign ministry has been sent to the WHO. (https://borkena.com/2022/01/13/tedros-adhanom-threatening-moral-integrity-of-who-says-ethiopia/)

Tedros, in the past, has said that he will not take sides in the conflict, but as the most high-profile Tigrayan, he may be under tremendous pressure air his views.

Ethiopia's Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed, the Nobel Peace Prize winner of 2019 is leading his country’s efforts to wrest territory from the Tigrayan rebels who had reached almost the outskirts of Addis Ababa, the Ethiopian capital.
