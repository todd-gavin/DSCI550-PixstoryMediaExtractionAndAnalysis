People who got COVID have a higher risk of developing brain disorders such as dementia psychosis and brain fog said a study in Lancet The study says the effects would be seen in people two years after they were infected than people who had other respiratory illnesses University of Oxford researchers examined medical data on about million people diagnosed with COVID from Jan and April and compared it to data from an equal number of people who had other respiratory diseases Most of the patients were from the United States Researchers said it was encouraging that the risk of anxiety and depression was transient for COVID patients with the risk dropping sharply two months after infection
