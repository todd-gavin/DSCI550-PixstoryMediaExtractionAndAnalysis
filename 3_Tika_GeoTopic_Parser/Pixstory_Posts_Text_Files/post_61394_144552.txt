Souffl Souffl is a very popular baked egg-based delicacy of French origin It was invented in the early 18th century It can contain a variety of ingredients and can be served as a savory main course or as a sweet dessert base ingredients include flavored pastry cream, a cream or puree sauce that serves as the base, and beaten egg whites that make the souffle puffy and fluffy They are deliciously light and airy with a subtle egg flavor The texture is golden and crispy on the outside and creamy on the inside In the middle of the 19th century, chef Marie Antonie Careme perfected and popularized it
