In a coordinated move, the United States and 30 other countries have decided to release 60m barrels from their strategic reserves. The announcement came when the International crude prices touched a seven-year high. 

West Texas Intermediate crude futures,  jumped to $106.50 a barrel and has stayed there. But experts say that the US has not been able to inflict maximum pain on Russia because of its leverage in the world energy market. This could be a major worry for governments around the world.

The US may be the world's largest producer of crude oil and natural gas, but prices are set in the international market and every increase will affect the consumers. If the war continues and Russia pushes hard, oil prices could rise to $130 and more within no time, experts predict. (https://www.ft.com/content/35d50bc3-bc44-48a3-9e7d-98dc48a901db)

Although the US and the EU have not touched energy directly in their sanctions, other measures are likely to warn companies dealing with Russia that dealing with Putin government is fraught with dangers. The West is hoping that the Russian economy could be hit hard even without touching the energy exports on which many European powers depend. But if doesn't happen, Putin could have greater power. (https://edition.cnn.com/2022/03/01/business/russia-economy-sanctions/index.html)

Meanwhile, the White House has said that it was prepared for more sanctions. "We are prepared to use every tool available to us to limit disruption to global energy supply as a result of President Vladimir Putin's actions," White House spokeswoman Jen Psaki said on Tuesday. She also said that Biden Administration would make all efforts to look for new sources to go down dependence on Russia. 9https://www.bbc.com/news/business-60557077)

Talks with Iran in Vienna on its nuclear programme is also likely to affect oil prices. If the negotiations fail, there could be more pressure on oil prices.
