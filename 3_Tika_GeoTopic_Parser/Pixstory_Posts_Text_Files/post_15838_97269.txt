Pedro L Valenzuela PhD Alejandro Lucia MD Introduction Whether diabetes is associated with hypertension risk remains controversial potentially owing to the confounding effect of lifestyle This study aims to analyze the association between diabetes and hypertension in adults and the mediating impacts of lifestyle Methods A cohort of Spanish workers aged years insured by an occupational risk prevention company participated in this nationwide cross sectional study between and data analysis was performed in Participants lifestyle related factors BMI sleeping hours alcohol smoking and physical activity were assessed and the prevalence of hypertension and diabetes was registered Results A total of participants women aged SD years with diabetes and with hypertension with complete data for all variables were assessed Having diabetes was associated with a higher prevalence of hypertension even after adjusting for all lifestyle related factors OR CI and people with diabetes and hypertension had a higher prevalence of mild kidney function impairment than people with diabetes alone OR CI However people with diabetes and an optimal lifestyle normal weight and sleeping hours absent to little alcohol drinking nonsmoking and regular physical activity presented a prevalence of hypertension comparable with that of those without diabetes OR CI In separate analyses among people with diabetes an optimal lifestyle was associated with a lower prevalence of hypertension than the worse opposite lifestyle OR CI The lifestyle related factors showing the strongest inverse association with adjusted risk of hypertension were normal weight OR CI versus overweight obesity and regular physical activity OR CI versus inactivity Conclusions Diabetes is positively and largely independently associated with hypertension risk Yet a healthy lifestyle can attenuate this association Sources
