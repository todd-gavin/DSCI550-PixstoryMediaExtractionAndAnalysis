Same gender companionship is a common occurrence among the albatrosses of remote Antipodes Islands a tiny volcanic chain miles southwest of New Zealand as birds struggle to find opposite gender partners Due to industrial fishing near the female s northern feeding grounds many female albatrosses are caught in the nets and die causing males to outnumber females three to one As a result males are left with fewer potential female counterparts and those who are unable to search them stay together in same gender relationships In the most recent episode of Sir David Attenborough s acclaimed series Frozen Planet II a pair of same gender albatrosses of same island are found making love with each other
