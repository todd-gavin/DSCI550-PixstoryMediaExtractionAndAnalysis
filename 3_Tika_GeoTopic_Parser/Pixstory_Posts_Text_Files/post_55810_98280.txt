Nam Le is an Australian writer of Vietnamese descent who won the Dylan Thomas Prize for his collection of short stories The Boat Best Australian Stories Best New American Voices Zoetrope All Story A Public Space and One Story are just a few of the places where his stories have appeared Despite the fact that Nam Le began his career as a lawyer the subjects of his writings are far from local The Boat is a large book featuring stories about the suffocation of theocracy in Iran Tehran Calling a child assassin in Colombia Cartagena and a New York artist seeking to reunite with his daughter Meeting Elise An unsettling reunion between a young Vietnamese writer in America and his ex soldier father and the title tale which depicts a group of tired refugees fleeing the Vietcong on a stricken boat serve as bookends to the anthology The release of Le s upcoming work is keenly anticipated
