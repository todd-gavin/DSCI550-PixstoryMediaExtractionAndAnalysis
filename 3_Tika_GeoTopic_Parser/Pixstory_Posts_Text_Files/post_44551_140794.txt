The Sino Indian Boundary Issue continues Maj Relingnao Bob Khating born in Ukhrul Manipur into the Tangkhul Naga community was a batch mate of Gen T N Raina s later to become Army Chief and Lt Gen J F R Jacob s at the O T S Mhow He ws commissioned into the th Hyderabad Regiment which was later renamed the Kumaon Regiment and posted to the th Battalion then at the regiment s Training Centre in Agra where he served as a Company Officer under the Company Commander Major K S Thimayya who was later to become Army Chief While serving in the V force in Burma behind the Japanese lines in WW he was awarded the Military Cross for gallantry the Army Chief s Gallantry Certificate and twice Mentioned in Despatches and was later made a Member of the British Empire MBE After the War Capt Bob Khating MC MBE was transferred to the Assam Regiment and joined its rd Battalion in Shillong as a Company Commander After India became independent on August on repeated requests from the Maharajah of Manipur the Political Agent and the Ukhrul elders to return to Manipur to serve the State he finally resigned from the Army and in October joined the Government of Manipur State as Minister in charge of the Hills Administration After winning the subsequent elections he continued as Minister in Charge of Hills Administration and also raised the Manipur Rifles the State Armed Police Force When the Raja of Manipur acceded to India in October and Manipur became part of the Indian Union he was requested to temporarily join the Assam Rifles till he could be absorbed into the newly forming Indian Frontier Administration Service IFAS under the Ministry of External Affairs As an IFAS officer he was awarded the Padmashree in for his outstanding work in establishing Indian administration in various parts of Arunachal Pradesh and for his relief work after the devastating earthquake of August The Sino Indian Boundary Issue Concluded Next up An Overview of the War Chapter
