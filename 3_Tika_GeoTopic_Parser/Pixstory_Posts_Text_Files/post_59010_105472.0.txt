IMD Issues 'Orange Alert' Weather Updates, Predicting Severe Heatwave For Delhi-NCR According to IMD, the monsoon season in Delhi is still a long way off, and the department is keeping a close eye on the situation.

The Indian Meteorological Department (IMD) issued a 'orange alert' across Delhi and neighbouring districts of Haryana, Punjab, and Uttar Pradesh on Monday, citing rising temperatures and extreme heatwaves. The severe heat wave will last four more days in Delhi, Haryana, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, and parts of Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan, according to the weather service.

"In Delhi, an orange alert has been issued. From June 4 to June 8, Haryana, Punjab, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, and parts of MP and Rajasthan are experiencing extreme heat. Temperatures are expected to fluctuate between 44 and 47 degrees Celsius for the next four days. On Monday, prominent IMD scientist RK Jenamani remarked, "We encourage people to venture out carefully because the heat spell is really intense."

"Isolated heatwave conditions are predicted over Jammu, Himachal Pradesh, West Rajasthan, north Jharkhand, and Vidarbha till Tuesday; Odisha, Uttar Pradesh, and Madhya Pradesh until Wednesday; Punjab, Haryana, Delhi, and Uttarakhand until June 9," according to the weather forecast department.

Heavy rain is anticipated in the north-eastern areas of the country. Warnings have been issued for Assam, Meghalaya, and Arunachal Pradesh.
