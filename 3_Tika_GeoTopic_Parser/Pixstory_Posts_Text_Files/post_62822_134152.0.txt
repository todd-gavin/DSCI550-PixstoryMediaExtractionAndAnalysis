1. Wild: From Lost to Found on the Pacific Crest Trail by Cheryl Strayed
This book tells the story of Cheryl Strayed's 2,000-mile journey along the Pacific Crest Trail, which starts in California and winds its way all the way to Oregon. Along the way, she experiences a variety of wild landscapes and encounters various animals, including bears, elk, and mountain lions. The book is an excellent read if you're interested in nature or want to learn more about hiking trails.
2. Born to Run: A Hidden Tribe, Ultra-Runners and the Greatest Race the World Has Never Seen by Christopher McDougall
This book tells the story of endurance runners who live on Type 1 diabetes primitive diets that allow them to run long distances without getting tired. McDougall profiles some of these runners (including an American sailor who ran around Cape Horn) and their amazing tales of running far beyond what humans are supposed to be able to do. If you're interested in trail Running or ultra running, this is a must-read book! 
 3. The Innovator's Dilemma: When New Technologies Cause Great Firms to Fail by Clayton M. Christensen
In this book, Christensen argues that new technologies (like computers and smartphones) are always disruptive and cause great firms to fail because they can't keep up with the competition.
