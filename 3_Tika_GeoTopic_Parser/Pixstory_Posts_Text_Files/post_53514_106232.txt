As Kenya marked its th anniversary of internal self rule on June a controversial play by the nation s foremost author Ng g wa Thiong o was staged in sold out shows It had been years since it was banned and the author detained The performance offers a useful filter to illuminate how the nation has fared in recent years Democracy is gradually taking root but corruption is still rife This makes Kenya s largely youthful population restive Without a doubt Ngaahika Ndeenda I Will Marry When I Want is the most consequential piece of writing by Ng g wa Thiong o and his collaborator the late Ng g wa Mirii The drama tells the story of Kiguunda a peasant whose tiny strip of earth is being targeted by Ahab Kioi a local tycoon who represents international financial interests Using multiple story threads the play captures the tempestuous romance between Kiguunda s daughter and Kioi s son which results in an unwanted pregnancy and a bleak future Kiguunda s delusion of a white wedding as social leverage leads to nothing but mockery and dispossession Within months of its writing and subsequent staging in late Ng g was detained without trial Under Kenya s old constitution which was replaced by a more progressive one in it was lawful for the president to detain anyone without trial Although the reason for Ng g s detention has never been given he told me recently its timing affirmed he had been targeted for writing in his indigenous language Gikuyu I thought Wait a minute I have been writing in English over the years and nobody ever bothered with me I write one play in Gikuyu and I m detained so I m going to write in Gikuyu Ng g spent a year at the Kamiti Maximum Security Prison His detention helped shine a light on Kenya s human rights record It also shaped his life in writing and political activism Read more
