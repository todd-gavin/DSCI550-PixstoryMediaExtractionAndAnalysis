Mateship with Birds by Carrie Tiffany is set in rural Australia in the s in the town of Cohuna in northern Victoria Betty works as a nurse in a retirement home and lives with her two children on a farm not far from town next door to a dairy farmer named Harry whose wife has run off with a bird fanciers club member Tiffany delves into lives bursting with the practicalities of operating a farm as well as a desire to learn about and experience the world around them It s a story about love adventure and acceptance as well as the strange ways desire can emerge This book also won the inaugural Stella Prize It is a hymn to the rhythm of country life to vicious birds virginal cows adored dogs and ill used sheep On one small farm in a vast ancient landscape a collection of misfits question the nature of what a family can be
