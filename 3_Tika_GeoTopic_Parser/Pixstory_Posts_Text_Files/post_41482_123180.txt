Many students start a new school year by participating in their old sports or trying something new According to Dr Theodore Shybut assistant professor of orthopaedic surgery and sports medicine at Baylor College of Medicine in Houston encouraging children to engage in physical activity has long term benefits US News reported that for children starting fall sports or any physical activities at any age Shybut offered some advice His words of wisdom come at a time when many kids could be losing interest in team sports Shybut advises allowing young children the chance to try a variety of activities to determine which ones they enjoy the most Create a setting where a youngster feels inspired to be active by engaging in regular free play at home going on family hikes or using the latest trends
