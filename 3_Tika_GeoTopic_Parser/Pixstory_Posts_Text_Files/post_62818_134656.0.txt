If you're interested in starting out at young adult fiction, then there are a few books that you should definitely read. They include The Catcher in the Rye by J.D. Salinger, The Outsiders by S.E. Hinton, The  The Maze Runner by James Dashner, Divergent by Veronica Roth  and To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee.
The Maze Runner. In this dystopian novel, Thomas leads a group of young adults who have been taken prisoner by an unknown organization known as The Glade. They must use their skills and intellect to escape the maze-like structure and find freedom.
Divergent. In this novel, Tris is trying to find out who she is and where she fit in the world. She's part of a society where people are divided into five factions based on their personality traits. When her faction finds out she's Divergent, they decide to send her off to training to learn how to be like everyone else. However, Tris decides that she wants to be unique and discovers that there might be more than one way to be brave and strong.
The Catcher in the Rye is a story about Holden Caulfield, who is trying to find his place in the world after experiencing great loss. He's initially rejected by society, but he eventually learns to trust his own judgment and rebel against society's norms.
The Outsiders is set during the 1950s and tells the story of two boys - Ponyboy and Johnny - who live on the outskirts of town and are constantly vying for supremacy over each other. Ponyboy is born into a poor family but rises to become one of the most successful members of his school community thanks to his fierce loyalty and sense of justice.
To Kill a Mockingbird tells the story of Scout Finch, an African American girl living in Alabama during the 1930s. She grows up witnessing racial discrimination firsthand, which makes her determined to fight for justice even when no one else will do it for her.
