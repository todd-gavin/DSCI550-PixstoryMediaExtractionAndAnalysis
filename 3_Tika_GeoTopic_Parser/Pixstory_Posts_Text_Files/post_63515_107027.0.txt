Central Chile’s Penuelas reservoir used to be Valparaiso’s main source of water. Now, it is a graveyard of death, a ghost of the haunting present: “A huge expanse of dried and cracked earth that was once the lake bed is littered with fish skeletons and desperate animals searching for water,” Reuters reports.

How do we grapple with the reality of lakes turning to deserts? How does a parched throat communicate the harshness that will increasingly exacerbate for the next 50 years, regardless of the quick fix cough drops we take?
