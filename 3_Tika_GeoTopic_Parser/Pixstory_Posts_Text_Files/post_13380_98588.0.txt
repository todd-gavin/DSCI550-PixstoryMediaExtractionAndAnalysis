The Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO) says the country is facing severe hunger due to the long wars in Afghanistan.

The agency says the number of people starving in various countries has reached 193 million due to war, climate change and economic problems.

A report released by the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations on Wednesday (May 14th) states that global hunger will increase significantly unless immediate and immediate humanitarian action is taken.

The UN Humanitarian Coordinator for Afghanistan says the number of hungry people in the country has risen from 14 million in January 2021 to 23 million in March 2022.

The agency says a whole generation of Afghans is at risk.

Although Afghanistan has been in a bad situation in the past due to severe drought, Taliban domination has led the country to move more rapidly towards economic collapse and poverty.
