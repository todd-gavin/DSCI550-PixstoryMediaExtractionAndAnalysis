Ami Fukunaga Seitaro Dohi Abstract Objective To examine the association of diabetes and prediabetes with suicide deaths in a Japanese working population Methods A nested case control study was conducted using data from the Japan Epidemiology Collaboration on Occupational Health Study During years of follow up we identified registered cases of suicide death that had information on fasting plasma glucose or glycated hemoglobin A c at any health check up during the past years prior to suicide For each case we randomly selected five controls matched for age sex and worksite We used the latest health check up data for analysis We defined diabetes status based on the American Diabetes Association criteria and used a conditional logistic regression model to investigate the association Results Adjusted odds ratios and confidence intervals for suicide death were and for prediabetes and diabetes respectively compared to normoglycemia Similar results were obtained when diabetes status was exclusively defined by the fasting plasma glucose or glycated hemoglobin A c level Conclusion Diabetes but not prediabetes was associated with a higher risk of suicide death compared with normoglycemia among a Japanese working population Sources
