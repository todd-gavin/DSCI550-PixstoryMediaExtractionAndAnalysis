UNICEF in coordination with UN partner agencies and de facto authorities led by the Ministry of Defense is rapidly preparing a multi-sectoral response covering health, water and sanitation, child protection, nutrition, education, social mobilization and including cash component UNICEF to conduct rapid assessment of market conditions in affected areas in coming days Assessment will determine feasibility and adequacy of cash as a response to support early recovery UNICEF has already trained partners on beneficiary registration on the ground In the water and sanitation sector, the response includes the provision of soap and water treatment products to mitigate the increased risk of an acute watery diarrhea outbreak cholera UNICEF will also respond with emergency latrines repairs of damaged water systems and emergency water transport On child protection, UNICEF is activating community-based child protection networks in affected areas and will increase the number of child protective service providers and social workers
