The Catcher in the Rye by J.D. Salinger

 Holden Caulfield is a 16-year-old boy who's kicked out of boarding school and has nothing to look forward to but his next trip across America, which he plans to do in a stolen car. Along the way, he meets a group of eccentric characters, some of whom he likes and some of whom he doesn't - but one thing is for sure: they're all going to change his life for the better. 

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

 Scout is a young girl growing up in Alabama during the 1930s. She's forced to grow up too fast when her father is arrested and her brother is sent away to live with their white family. She still finds time to be an incorrigible tomboy and learn about life from her father, Atticus Finch.

The Girl with the Dragoon Tattoo by Stieg Larsson 
This crime novel follows Lisbeth Salander, an investigative journalist and hacker, as she investigates a series of murders that involve wealthy businesspeople.

The Da Vinci Code by Dan Brown

In this thriller, Brown introduces readers to Robert Langdon, an academic who travels to Paris to solve the mystery behind the Holy Grail.

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone by J.K. Rowling 

When Harry Potter learns he is a wizard, he sets out on a journey to find and defeat Lord Voldemort, who wants to kill him and everyone else in Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

  The Hunger Games by Suzanne Collins 

Collins' dystopian novel follows Katniss Everdeen as she leads her fellow tributes in a televised battle to the death in order to save humanity from the tyranny of President Snow.
