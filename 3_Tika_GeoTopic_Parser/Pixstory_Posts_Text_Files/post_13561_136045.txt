Reuters news agency reported citing an American official that the United States will stop the temporary transfer of Afghans to this country with the exception of a few cases From now on America will focus on reunification of Afghan families and their permanent residence This policy reform follows the criticism and efforts of some legislators human rights organizations and the defense of asylum seekers Since the fall of the previous government Afghans have been transferred to the United States The revised policy of facilitating the stay of Afghans in America will be implemented from October According to the US official the revised policy will focus on the relocation of Afghan family members with US citizenship green card holders and Afghans with special immigrant visas at risk from the Taliban Providing long term immigration status to family members of admitted individuals allows them to settle more quickly and become familiar with the new community
