The Black Lives Matter Mural Submission Exhibition a collaboration between the Sunny Cortland university s Institutional Equity and Inclusion Office Dowd Gallery the Art and Art History Department and the Black Lives Matter Mural Committee debuted on Feb and is free to the public during building hours Following a competition that drew online entries three graphic design and digital media majors were chosen as project artists in December At the end of this semester a bold four panel BLM mural designed by students to reflect the national struggle against racial and social injustice will be installed in the newly remodelled Moffett Center Forum Meanwhile on Thursday March the winning illustrators will be celebrated at an opening event in the Colloquium which is right across the street The collection of pieces celebrates and honors individuals who found inspiration in Black Lives Matter and wanted to share their ideas with the campus community
