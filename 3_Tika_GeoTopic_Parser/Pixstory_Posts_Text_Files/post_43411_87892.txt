Ingredients cup Refined flour tbs Active dry yeast tsp Sugar cloves Garlic finely chopped Salt Luke Warm Water For Filling Onion chopped Coriander leaves roughly torn Mint Leaves cloves Garlic Salt to taste tsp Red chilli powder tsp Turmeric powder cup Butter Ghee for basting tsp Black Sesame seeds Coriander leaves for garnish Method For the dough take refined flour in a bowl Add salt to it and mix In another bowl take dry yeast and add warm water to it Add in sugar and mix Let it rest for minutes Now make a well in the middle of the flour Pour the activated yeast mixture Also add chopped garlic Mix everything together Use lukewarm water to knead soft and smooth dough Cover and let it rest for an hour Meanwhile for the stuffing take chopped onion in a bowl and add mint coriander garlic to it Mix everything well Now take the dough and knead it once again to release out the trapped air Divide the dough into small round balls according to the required size of the kulcha Roll out the ball into small circle and add a tablespoon of the mixture into the center Bring the corners of the dough together and seal it Flatten it again by gently pressing it in between your palm Dust it with flour and roll it out Apply little water on the surface of the kulcha and sprinkle black sesame seeds and coriander leaves on top and give it a gentle press Heat the iron skillet and place the prepared kulcha on it Cook on both the sides until it starts to puff up Once puffed directly cook it over the heat and keep flipping it Let it cook evenly and do not burn the kulcha Once cooked remove it on a plate and apply butter or ghee and serve hot
