Monty Norman best known for composing the iconic score of the James Bond movies died on July at the age of A statement posted Monday on Norman s official website said It is with sadness we share the news that Monty Norman died on th July after a short illness Norman was born on April as Monty Noserovitch in east London to a Jewish family However his most remembered piece was his Bond theme which was commissioned in for James Bond debut Dr No which was used throughout the series For the main theme the composer used the structure of one of his earlier compositions Good Sign Bad Sign from an abandoned musical production of VS Naipaul s novel A House For Mr Biswas and re wrote it using the gentleman womanizing assassin in mind After switching the main riff from a sitar to an electric guitar Norman said that he knew he at that point that he had captured the essence of His sexiness his mystery his ruthlessness it s all there in a few notes he later went on to recall His music for Dr No also includes the song Underneath The Mango Tree which he taught to Sean Connery and Ursula Andress and a theme that accompanies the opening scene with the three blind assassins In a varied career Norman also sang with big bands and also appeared in variety shows alongside the likes of Harry Secombe Peter Sellers Spike Milligan and Tommy Cooper He also wrote the music for the Hammer movie The Two Faces of Dr Jekyll The Day the Earth Caught Fire the Bob Hope Eon Productions movie Call Me Bwana and the TV miniseries Dickens of London
