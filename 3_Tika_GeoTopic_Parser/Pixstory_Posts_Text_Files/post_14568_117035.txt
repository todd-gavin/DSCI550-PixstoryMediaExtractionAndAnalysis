Gaining a better understand of the brain based drivers and consequences of hedonic overeating is critical given the fact that some people are able to maintain a healthy weight despite the omnipresence of high fat and high sugar foods while others experience unhealthy weight gain Recent advances in human neuroimaging techniques have revealed obesity dependent patterns of aberrant neural response to food stimuli However there remains disagreement regarding underlying causes and neural consequences of overeating and weight gain These lead to obesity and diabetes and then to Non Alchoholic Fatty Liver Disease NAFLD and Metabolic Associated Fatty Liver Disease MAFLD Specifically we examine possible hedonic eating risk factors brain based predictors of weight gain correlates of eating behavior and neural consequences of weight gain and obesity
