London on Friday approved an extradition order for Julian Assange the embattled founder of WikiLeaks upholding a court decision that he can be sent to the United States to stand trial on spying charges A decision that comes as a major blow to Assange whose case is seen by human rights groups as a potential challenge to press freedom Assange now has days to appeal decision in UK court Home Office comments favorably on UK court decision there is no oppression it is not an unfair decision and there is no finds abuse of process to extradite Mr Assange Furthermore, judges believe extradition would be compatible with his human rights including his right to a fair trial and freedom of expression and that while he is in the US he will be treated appropriately also in relation to his health
