Every summer as the sea ice surrounding Antarctica retreats tens of thousands of tourists and scientists flock to the landmass by boat and plane The remote continent is becoming increasingly accessible during the season the number of sightseeing visitors reached with the vast majority travelling by ship Scientific activities on the continent are also significant with more than research stations collectively housing thousands of researchers This activity which is projected to increase in future leaves a physical footprint with lasting consequences In seeking to study or marvel at one of the last nearly undisturbed places on earth humans are having a growing impact that can be measured and quantified Under the Antarctic Treaty tourist and scientific operators are required to remove waste from the continent Trash and human waste are flown or shipped off the continent for disposal at warmer latitudes But some forms of waste are not so easily squirrelled off the continent All activity in Antarctica be it powered drills for scientific ice coring or vehicles for transport burns fuel As we burn fuel to keep warm or to move around our activities release microscopic particles of black carbon smoke and soot Read full story at The Conversation Imagery Foto shutterstock
