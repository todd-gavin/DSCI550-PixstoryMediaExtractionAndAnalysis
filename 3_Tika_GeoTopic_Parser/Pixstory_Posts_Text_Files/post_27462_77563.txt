The positive epilogue of the Formula season could have given Honda the necessary impetus for a breakthrough After deciding to leave the World Championship at the end of the championship then won by Max Verstappen, the Japanese engineer could remain the first-person power unit supplier to the young Dutch world champion A choice dictated by the fact that the engines will be developed once again before the start of the season and then be frozen until with clear savings in terms of costs This hypothesis was proposed by Red Bull manager Helmut Marko recently interviewed by the Austrian magazine Autorevue in this way the Milton Keynes team would have even more time to stay on the sidelines and prepare a future as an engine maker tout court or a possible agreement for the with the Volkswagen group about to announce its arrival in the top automotive series
