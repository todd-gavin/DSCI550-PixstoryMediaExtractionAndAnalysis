Palazzo Fortuny, the Gothic Venetian palace that was the home and workshop of Mariano Fortuny y Madrazo Granada Venezia and his muse, wife and companion Henriette Nigrin, reopens in Venice from March and production center in the cosmopolitan and industrious Venice reopens its doors with an overall rearrangement of the noble floors and a new destiny no longer just a temporary exhibition space but a permanent museum in which Mariano Fortuny and his universe of light and innovation are once again the protagonists For the first two days Saturday and Sunday in March the Museum will be open to the public with free admission and reservations required
