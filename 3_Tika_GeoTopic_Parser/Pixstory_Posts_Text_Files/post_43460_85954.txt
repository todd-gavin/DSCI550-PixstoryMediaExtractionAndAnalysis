When insect wings are glistening in the beam Of the low sun and mountain tops are bright Oh let me by the crystal valley stream Wander amid the mild and mellow light And while the redbreast pipes his evening lay Give me one lonely hour to hymn the setting day Oh sun that o er the western mountains now Goest down in glory ever beautiful And blessed is thy radiance whether thou Colourest the eastern heaven and night mist cool Till the bright day star vanish or on high Climbest and streamest thy white splendours from mid sky Yet loveliest are thy setting smiles and fair Fairest of all that earth beholds the hues That live among the clouds and flush the air Lingering and deepening at the hour of dews Wiliam Cullen Bryant
