There could be some signs insinuating the presence of the syndrome or the beginning.
Like anything else, we need signs to know things accurately, and so is the case with this syndrome.
Imposter syndrome is frequently associated with trait anxiety, generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), and social anxiety disorder. 
Pervasive self-doubt characterizes your past, current, and future experiences.
Despite objective successes, you experience a persistent fear that you will be “found out” or discovered as a fraud.
When you achieve success, you attribute it to luck or describe it as a fluke. As a result, you might feel relief or distress in place of happiness and pride.
You look for validation in authority figures—such as a boss or family member—and give them the power to dictate whether you are successful.
