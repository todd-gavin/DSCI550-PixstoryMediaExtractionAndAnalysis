Monkeypox is the latest global public health threat to make headlines Most people who contract the monkeypox virus experience flu like symptoms and a blistery rash that lasts two to four weeks but a small percentage of infected people develop sepsis or other severe and potentially fatal complications It is not uncommon for there to be small outbreaks of monkeypox in Central and West Africa but in number of cases being diagnosed is large enough to qualify as epidemic An epidemic is characterized by new cases of a disease occurring at a higher than typical rate in at least several communities
