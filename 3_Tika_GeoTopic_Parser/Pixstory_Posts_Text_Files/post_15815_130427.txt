ABSTRACT Objectives Inadequate social support make way for peer support initiatives to complement the health system delivery of diabetes self management education programs for type diabetes T D This review synthesizes knowledge about T D peer support in terms of their various models and impact endorsement and contextual information in high income nations Methods A scoping review was conducted on published and grey literature in four electronic bibliographic databases between January to December Results records were included Face to face self management programs and telephone based peer support seem the most promising modalities given the largest scientific coverage on T D outcomes Face to face self management programs were the most preferred by ethnic minority groups Unlike peer supporters healthcare professionals had mixed views about T D peer support interventions Managers of peer support programs perceived cultural competency as a cornerstone for peer support implementation Care must be taken in recruiting training and retaining peer supporters for sustainable practice Conclusions Various T D peer support models lie at the interface between primary care and community based settings Sources
