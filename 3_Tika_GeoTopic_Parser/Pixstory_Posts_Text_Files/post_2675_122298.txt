Mankeshwar Temple in Nashik In Maharashtra was originally built in th Century CE but was destroyed by Aurangzeb and later again By Adil Shahis Somehow some of the original sculptures still survive This Sculpture was covered under Sindoor a bright red dye traditionally worn in the parting of the hair by married women in India Legend has it that by applying Sindoor on the Sculpture married life would be blessed But centuries of Blessings caused the original Sculpture to be loaded with Sindoor Archeological Survey of India finally got a Sculptor to clean the Sindoor off and the result is an intricate carving Specially the intertwined plaits
