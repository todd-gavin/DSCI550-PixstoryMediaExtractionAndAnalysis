It's hard to pinpoint whether anxiety or sleep disruptions comes first , but researchers found the relationship between sleep issues and anxiety is bidirectional.
Lack of sleep can cause/increase anxiety and anxiety disrupts sleep.
Anxiety and sleep issues often go hand in hand. With increased worry and fear, your body and mind are in a state of activation, making it harder to fall and stay asleep.
Missing out on quality sleep can amplify anxiety, creating a vicious cycle of sleep issues and anxiety. Research has shown that lack of sleep affects mood and emotional regulation.
By creating rituals and schedules that prioritise healthy, good quality sleep, you cancombat feelings of anxiety.
Your sleep habits, environment and daily schedule all from sleep hygiene that make bedtime a more pleasurable experience, allowing your body and mind to optimally recover.
