Chandigarh's symbol - Open Hand 
The Open Hand Monument is a symbolic structure designed by the architect Le Corbusier and located in the Capitol Complex of Chandigarh. It is the emblem and symbol of Chandigarh and symbolizes "the hand to give and the hand to take; peace and prosperity, and the unity of mankind".
The largest example of Le Corbusier's many Open Hand sculptures, it stands 26 metres (85 ft) high. The metal structure with vanes is 14 metres (46 ft) high, weighs 50 short tons (100,000 lb), and was designed to rotate in the wind.
