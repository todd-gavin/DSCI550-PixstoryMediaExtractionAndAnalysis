Italy manager Roberto Mancini has announced his official squad for the upcoming CONMEBOL-UEFA Cup of Champions final against Argentina and UEFA Nations League fixtures. 


The match against Argentina takes place on 1st June in London as a one-off clash organized by UEFA and CONEMBOL as a World Super Cup. Italy then face Germany, England and Hungary in the UEFA Nations League.


Here is the full squad below:


Goalkeepers: Alessio Cragno (Cagliari), Gianluigi Donnarumma (Paris Saint Germain), Alex Meret (Naples), Salvatore Sirigu (Genoa) make the cut with Donnarumma expected to be in goal despite his shaky form for PSG. 


Defenders: Francesco Acerbi (Lazio), Alessandro Bastoni (Inter), Cristiano Biraghi (Fiorentina), Leonardo Bonucci (Juventus), Davide Calabria (Milan), Giorgio Chiellini (Juventus), Giovanni Di Lorenzo (Naples), Federico Dimarco (Inter) , Emerson Palmieri Dos Santos (Olympique Lyonnais), Alessandro Florenzi (Milan), Manuel Lazzari (Lazio), Luiz Felipe (Lazio), Gianluca Mancini (Rome), Leonardo Spinazzola (Rome) have been called up by Mancini with Chiellini and Florenzi expected to deputise in the heart of defence. 


Midfielders: Nicolò Barella (Inter), Bryan Cristante (Rome), Davide Frattesi (Sassuolo), Jorginho Frello Jorge Luiz (Chelsea), Manuel Locatelli (Juventus), Lorenzo Pellegrini (Rome), Matteo Pessina (Atalanta), Sandro Tonali (Milan), Marco Verratti (Paris Saint Germain). Jorginho will be expected to be the conductor in the Italian midfield, hopefully being able to put the ignominy of missing the penalty which knocked Italy out of World Cup Qualifiers. 


Forwards: Andrea Belotti (Turin), Domenico Berardi (Sassuolo), Federico Bernardeschi (Juventus), Gianluca Caprari (Verona), Lorenzo Insigne (Naples), Moise Bioty Kean (Juventus), Andrea Pinamonti (Empoli), Matteo Politano (Naples) , Giacomo Raspadori (Sassuolo), Gianluca Scamacca (Sassuolo), Mattia Zaccagni (Lazio), Nicolò Zaniolo (Rome) with either Belloti or Insigne tasked with the role to spearhead the Italian attack.
