A little over a week back, Sri Lanka President Gotbaya Rajapaksa went on national television and apologised to his country's people for the growing economic crisis that has resulted in constant power cuts, extreme fuel shortage and rising unemployment. He blamed it on the foreign exchange crisis and said that the country should have faith in him to solve the problems.

But the problems have been only growing with the armed forces being called to cities to keep a close watch on the growing unrest in the gas stations over shortages. Even essential commodities are vanishing from the shelves of grocery stores. 

With international rating agencies warning that the Indian Ocean country is on the verge of defaulting on its debt, the political administration is scrambling for a deal with the International Monetary Fund. 

The president had called for an all-party conference which turned out be stormy with former Sri Lankan Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe taking the country's central bank governor for blaming previous governments for the crisis.
