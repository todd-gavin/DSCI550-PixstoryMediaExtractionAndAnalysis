The US Special Representative for Afghanistan has condemned the attack on a Sikh shrine in Kabul.

Expressing his condolences to the families of the victims, Thomas West tweeted that vulnerable minorities should be protected.
The Taliban said one Sikh citizen was killed and at least seven others were injured in the attack.
ISIS claimed responsibility for the attack.
Earlier, a number of countries and human rights organizations also condemned the attack on Afghan Sikhs.
Following the attack, India prioritized the issuance of electronic visas to more than 100 Sikh and Hindu citizens of Afghanistan. Indian officials say about 150 Hindu and Sikh nationalities live in Afghanistan who are under serious threat.
