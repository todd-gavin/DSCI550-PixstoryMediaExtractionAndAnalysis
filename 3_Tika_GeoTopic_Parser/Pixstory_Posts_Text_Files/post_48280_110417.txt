Birth control pills are hormonal contraceptives that prevent pregnancy Because it comes in pill form it is knwon as the pill Women take the pill once a day orally by mouth The pill works best when taken consistently at the same time each day However the pill does not protect against sexually transmitted infections such as HIV Most STDs are best protected by using a latex condom when having sexual intercourse or even oral sex How does the pill work Pregnancy occurs when sperm fertilizes an egg released by your ovary The fertilized egg attaches to the wall of the uterus where it grows into a baby Hormones in your body control the release of the egg from the ovary known as ovulation and prepare your body to receive the fertilized egg Hormonal contraceptives the pill patch and vaginal ring all include small traces of human made hormones estrogen and progestin These hormones interfere with your body s natural hormones in a variety of ways to prevent conception The hormonal contraceptive prevents the body from ovulating in most cases They also alter the cervical mucous to make it more difficult for sperm to pass through and discover an egg They can also prevent pregnancy by altering the womb s lining and making it thick and less likely for the fertilized egg to be implanted How do the two types of birth control pills work Combination pills or COCs They have two functions The first is to stop your body from ovulating This indicates that your ovaries will not produce an egg every month Second these pills make your cervical mucus thicker which prevents the sperm to travel to your uterus and fertilize an egg The thick mucus prevents the sperm from entering the uterus Progestin only pills or POPs or mini pills They primarily work by thickening cervical mucus and thinning the endometrium The endometrium is the uterine lining where an egg implants following fertilization If this lining is thinner it will be more difficult for an egg to implant preventing pregnancy from occurring Ovulation may also be prevented by POPs Sources
