PROCEDURE Preheat the oven to and use a thermometer to verify the oven temperature this is the key to a good creme br l e Add the whole fat milk and heavy whipping cream into a medium saucepan on low heat Add pure vanilla extract tablespoon or vanilla bean Bring the milk cream vanilla mixture to a boil while stirring well and then immediately turn off the heat Set the saucepan aside to cool for minutes Separate the four egg yolks and add them to a medium sized mixing bowl along with the granulated white sugar Mix together the egg yolks and sugar gently with a wooden spoon Do not use a whisk We do not want foaming When the mixture has cooled to lukewarm place a wire mesh sieve over the yolk sugar mixing bowl Gently mix everything togetherWhen the sugar has dissolved pour in the remaining mixture through the sieve and mix gently but thoroughly You do not want to create foaming Now carefully sieve pour the cr me mixture again into a measuring cup for easy pouring into your ramekins Place your ramekins pour equal amounts of cr me thru the fine wire mesh sieve and into each ramekin Cook the cr mes for minutes on the sturdy baking tray in the oven on the lower rack at The cr mes are cooked when they are firm on the outer edge and just slightly wobbly in the center Tap Test gently tap the side of the ramequins with your finger to see if the center of the creme wobbles When the cr mes pass the tap test remove the tray from the oven and allow the ramekins to cool to room temperature After minutes move them into the refrigerator for at least hours to set overnight is best When the custard is ready and just before serving take the cr me br l es out of the refrigerator and sprinkle a layer of brown sugar on top of the custard as evenly as possible Each ounce ramekin will need between teaspoon and tablespoon of brown sugar depending on the size of the ramekins and your own taste
