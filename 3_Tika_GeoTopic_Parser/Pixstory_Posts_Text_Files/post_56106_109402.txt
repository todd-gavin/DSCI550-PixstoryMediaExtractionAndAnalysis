A doggerel is a form of poetry that is irregular in rhythm and rhyme often intentionally for burlesque or comic effect Alternatively it can mean verse that has a tedious rhythm easy rhyme and cheap or trivial meaning Appearing since ancient times in the literature of many cultures doggerel is found in contemporary nursery rhymes Ogden Nash made a virtue of writing what seems to be doggerel but is certainly clever and delightful despite its apparent technical faults Hip hop lyrics have also explored the artful possibilities of doggerel Chaucer s Tale of Sir Thopas is written in this format Doggerel has been employed in most English comic verse from that of Samuel Butler and Jonathan Swift to the American poet Ogden Nash
