The use of tools improves the quality of nutrition On the one hand it allows to obtain a more balanced diet with a greater quantity of macronutrients that provide energy such as fats and carbohydrates On the other hand since on the days in which the capuchin monkeys use tools the amount of fiber is lower of the mix of macronutrients they ingest becomes more concentrated The intake of a smaller quantity of fibers allows the digestive system to absorb the nutrients more efficiently concludes the primatologist The use of strikers allows the capuchin monkeys to take on more calories and to improve the diet and it is conceivable that this also happened during our evolutionary history well before lithic tools from simple stones became much more elaborate
