Google doodle is celebrating the 104th birth anniversary of Indian Physicist Anna Mani today. 
She was one of the most renowned physicist and meteorologist in India, whose work contributed to accurate weather prediction.
Apart from weather forecasts, her work helped India succeed in renewable energy.
Anna Mani is considered a national icon of women's empowerment in the country as she broke the stereotypes by fighting the gender-based norms and excelling in a male-dominant field. Here's the inspiring story of Anna Mani's life.
She was born in a family of Syrian-Christians in Peerumade village of Kerala on August 23, 1918. 
She graduated in Physics and Chemistry from the prestigious Presidency College, Madras (now Chennai), and also got a research scholarship for the Indian Institue of Science (IISc). After that, she went to London's Imperial College to study Physics and later got inclined toward meteorological instruments.
In her career, she invented over 100 weather instruments for India, and later she produced instruments for measuring solar and wind energy.
In 1987, she became the Deputy Director General of the Indian Meteorological Department (IMD) and was awarded the Indian National Science Academy (INSA), K.R Ramanathan Medal, for her contributions to the country and science. On Aug 16, 2001 she passed away in Thiruvananthapuram.
Anna Mani's life is an inspiring journey for every patriotic Indian, she was a staunch supporter of the Civil Disobedience Movement and a Gandhian at heart. 
She was a patriotic Indian women, who contributed to the scientific achievements and success of her motherland.
