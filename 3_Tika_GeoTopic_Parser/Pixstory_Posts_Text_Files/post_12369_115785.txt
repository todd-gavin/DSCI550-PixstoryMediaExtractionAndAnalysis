Likes and dislikes for the many different tastes and flavors of foods vary tremendously between individuals Patterns of acceptance differ broadly across cultural groups but likewise each person develops idiosyncratic preferences Experience tailors food preferences to match one s environment Sweetness and fat are both fundamental drivers of palatability Though these have different developmental origins each retains some plasticity throughout life Hedonic responses to sweet and fat do not fully explain palatability since individuals also are also attracted to complex flavors which are not innately liked and become preferred only with experience Research with humans and animal models has identified several developmental events and associative learning processes involved in establishing and maintaining these preferences Since learned responses to tastes and flavors generally steer choice and promote intake of calorically dense foods understanding these learning mechanisms helps explain specific ways that the modern food environment promotes overeating
