Tim Winton a Western Australian who has received four Miles Franklin prizes has been recognised a Living Treasure by The National Trust He s published books and a few short tales all of which are heavily influenced by his native state of Western Australia s coast with the author noting that place is a crucial feature of his works This could be the driving force behind his environmental activism despite his dissatisfaction with life in the spotlight Booktopia says In Cloudstreet two country families move to the city after different disasters and they end up sharing a vast breathing shuddering joint named Cloudstreet where they rebuild their lives from the ground up They roister and rankle laugh and swear for twenty years till the roof becomes their home Critics say that Tim Winton s funny sprawling saga is an epic novel of love and acceptance Winner of the Miles Franklin and NBC awards in Australia Cloudstreet is a celebration of people places and rhythms which has fuelled imaginations world wide
