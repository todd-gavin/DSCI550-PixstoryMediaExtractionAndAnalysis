Fiction can be educational in a number of ways It can help us to learn about different cultures and societies it can teach us new skills and it can provide us with emotional relief Fiction can also be used to explore difficult issues that would be too difficult or emotionally challenging for students to learn about in a classroom setting Fictional Writings are all about overcoming adversity and growing as a person This is an important lesson that students can learn from it regardless of their actual religious beliefs or political views Fictional shows or books from different parts of the world are a great way to understand their culture and lifestyles It can provide emotional relief For example when readers are struggling with difficult emotions like sadness or anger they often turn to fiction for comfort This is because fiction is designed to be emotionally engaging it establishes a strong connection between the reader and the protagonist characters Consequently reading fiction can help you process your emotions in a safe and therapeutic environment
