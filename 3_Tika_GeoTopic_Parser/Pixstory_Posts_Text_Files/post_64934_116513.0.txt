As many as six pilgrims and a pony driver died of natural causes during the Amarnath yatra since yesterday, taking the overall death toll in the ongoing pilgrimage to 51, officials said on Saturday.
The death toll includes 17 yatris who lost their life in the July 8 flash flood.
So far, 49 yatris and two pony drivers have lost their life during the pilgrimage which started on June 30.
One pony driver had died after he fell from his horse into a deep gorge in Pahalgam.
In the July 8 flash flood near the cave shrine, 17 yatris had lost their life while about 50 persons were injured.
In all, about 1.5 lakh pilgrims visited the holy cave shrine in Kashmir after having 'darshan' of the naturally formed ice- Shivalingam
