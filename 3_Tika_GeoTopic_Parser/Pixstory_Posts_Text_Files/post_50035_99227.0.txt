War poetry refers to poems written on the plight of war. The cruelities, friendships, hopes tragedy of war are depicted in these poems. Some suggestions include

Laurence Binyon, ‘For the Fallen‘.

Charles Sorley, ‘When you see millions of the mouthless dead‘.

John McCrae, ‘In Flanders Fields‘.

Wilfred Owen, ‘Dulce et Decorum Est‘.

Siegfried Sassoon, ‘Dreamers‘.

Rupert Brooke, ‘The Soldier‘.

Philip Larkin, ‘MCMXIV‘.

Siegfried Sassoon, Suicide in the Trenches
