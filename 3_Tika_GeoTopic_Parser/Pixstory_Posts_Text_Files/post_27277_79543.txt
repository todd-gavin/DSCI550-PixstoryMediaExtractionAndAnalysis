Who said that the stars can only be seen in summer In Milan they are there all year round just go to the Planetarium It is a place of great charm in itself built in a project by the architect Piero Portaluppi inside the gardens of Porta Venezia today dedicated to the journalist Indro Montanelli The officially Civic building Ulrico Hoepli planetarium has a large dome and is equipped inside with an instrument also called a planetarium which projects and represents the image of the stars and their movements on the celestial vault Today the Milan Planetarium is largest existing in Italy and the one with the most intense activity calculated in over visitors per year In its more than seventy years of history it has been visited by an overall public estimated at around million people
