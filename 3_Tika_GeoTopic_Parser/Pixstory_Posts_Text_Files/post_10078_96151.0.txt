The Islamic State (IS) has claimed responsibility for the attack in Afghanistan on Thursday that killed at least 31 people.

IS has claimed responsibility for the four explosions across Afghanistan on Thursday, April 21, in which at least 31 people were killed and 87 wounded.

One of the blasts was carried out at a mosque in Mazar-e-Sharif. At least 10 people were killed and dozens injured in the blast. According to ANI, the Taliban has condemned the blasts which were carried out in Kabul, Balkh, and Kunduz.

The first blast occurred in a public square in Kabul's Police District 5, in which many, including 2 children, were injured. The second blast occurred at Mazar-e-Sharif, the capital of Balkh province while the third explosion was reported from Kunduz.
