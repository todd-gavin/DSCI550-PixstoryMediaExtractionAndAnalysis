NFHS surveys from 2006 to 2019 have found a consistently increasing incidence of spousal violence in India. Domestic violence is a criminal offence under section 498-A of the Indian Penal Code and the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 was introduced to provide immediate relief to women in a household who is subjected to abuse by their husbands or in-laws. Yet domestic abuse is still widely prevalent in India. According to the Gender Inequality Index, India ranks 125 with every 1 in 3 women having been subjected to violence by their partners and National Crime Records Bureau data reported a total of 32,033 rape cases in 2019 in India.
