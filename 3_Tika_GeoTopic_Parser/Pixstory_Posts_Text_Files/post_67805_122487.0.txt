Sindhu Sriharsha

Sindhu currently serves as the captain of the United States women's cricket team. She started playing cricket at the age of nine in her hometown of Bangalore, Karnataka, and went on to represent the India A and India under-21 teams before moving to Livermore, California. 

The right-handed wicketkeeper batter made her debut for the United States against Canada in the Americas Qualifier in May 2019, and she made an immediate impact, becoming the series' leading run-scorer with 80 runs from three matches. 

Three months later, she was named captain of the United States WT20I team. She has played 11 matches for the USA since then, scoring 159 runs with a best score of 42 not out.
