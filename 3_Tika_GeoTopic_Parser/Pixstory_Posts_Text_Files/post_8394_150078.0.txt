Nine months after the demise of India's first ever CDS Bipin Rawat in a helicopter crash, The Government of India on Wednesday appointed Lt. Gen. Anil Chauhan (retd.) as the next Chief of Defense Staff (CDS). He will also function as Secretary to the Government of India, Department of Military Affairs, the Defense Ministry said.
Chauhan, a PVSM, UYSM, AVSM, SM, and VSM, has served as the Commanding General Officer in Chief of the Eastern Command.
According to the Ministry of Defense, in his nearly 40-year career, Chauhan held various command, staff and instrumental positions and had widespread experience in counter-insurgency operations in Jammu and Kashmir and northeast India.
Meet the new CDS:
Anil Chauhan was born on May 18, 1961, and got commissioned into the 11 Gorkha Rifles of Indian Army in 1981.
He is an alumnus of the NDA, Khadakwasla and the Indian Military Academy, Dehradun. At the rank of Major General, he had commanded an Infantry Division in the critical sector of Baramula in the Northern Command.
Later, as a Lt. Gen, he commanded a corps in the Northeast and subsequently became a commanding general officer in chief of the eastern command from September 2019 and held the position until his retirement from service in May 2021.
In addition to these command appointments, the officer also held important staff positions, including the position of Director General of Military Operations (DGMO).
