BRAILLE SYSTEM Braille is a universally accepted writing system used by and for people with visual impairments It consists of a code of sixty three characters These are read by lightly running the fingers over the surface of the manuscript Louis Braille invented the system in N Paris, France he went blind at the age of three The braille system was widely accepted Two years after the disappearance of braille the system was officially adopted in schools in Paris in braille are formed using a combination of six raised dots arranged in a matrix called a braille cell Braille allows people who are blind to access spelling, punctuation and other parts of written language new technologies such as screen-reading software that reads text aloud
