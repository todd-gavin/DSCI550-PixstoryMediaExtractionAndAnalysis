Hongjiang Wu Andrea O Y Luk Abstract Aims We aimed to conduct a systematic review of published studies on the incidence of type diabetes in children and adolescents aged under years and provide worldwide incidence estimates for Methods We used MEDLINE and EMBASE to identify studies reporting type diabetes incidence in children and adolescents published between Jan and April We used a negative binomial regression model to develop a prediction equation to estimate incidence rates from country characteristics We applied the resulting incidence predictions to country population data to estimate numbers of incident cases in children and adolescents by International Diabetes Federation IDF region and World Bank income classification group Results We estimate that there are approximately new cases of diagnosed type diabetes among children and adolescents in worldwide Around and of the worldwide total incident cases are in IDF Western Pacific region and in World Bank upper middle income countries respectively The three countries with the highest estimated number of incident cases are China India and United States of America Conclusions The number of newly diagnosed type diabetes in children and adolescents is substantial More reliable data are needed to track the incidence of type diabetes in children and adolescents Sources
