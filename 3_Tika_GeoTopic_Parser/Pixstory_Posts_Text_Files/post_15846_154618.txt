Abstract Objective To examine the associations between mental health and lifestyle in adults with type and type diabetes mellitus T DM and T DM Methods Online survey data from the cross sectional Diabetes MILES The Netherlands Study was analysed including adults with T DM and with T DM Mental health status flourishing moderate and languishing in relation to diet physical activity alcohol consumption and smoking was analysed with ANCOVA and logistic regressions adjusted for confounders Results of T DM and of T DM participants reported flourishing mental health Due to an insufficient number participants with languishing mental health were excluded In T DM participants with flourishing mental health had more optimal diet quality mean SEM vs diet quality score p and physical activity levels mean SEM vs MET minutes week p than those with moderate mental health but did not differ with respect to alcohol consumption and smoking In T DM no significant associations were found Conclusion Only in T DM people with flourishing mental health had more optimal lifestyle behaviours compared to people with moderate mental health Further research is needed to determine if mental health is more important for specific lifestyle behaviours and if the mental health effect differs across diabetes types Sources
