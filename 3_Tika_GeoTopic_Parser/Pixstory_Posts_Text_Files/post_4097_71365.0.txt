‌The Election Commission postponed the polls in Punjab to February 20, originally scheduled for February 14. 

‌There was a great persuasion of state government and political parties in the view Guru Ravidas Jayanti which falls on February 16.

‌ The parties pointed out that several followers of Guru Ravidas would not be able to fulfil their voting on February 14 as they would be travelling.

‌The new date overlaps with the third phase of polling in Uttar Pradesh.

‌Election results will be declared on 10 March 2022.

‌The Punjab Assembly has a total of 117 seats, with 59 being the majority mark. 

‌The 117 constituencies in Punjab are spread over three regions – Majha (25 constituencies), Doaba (23) and Malwa (69).
