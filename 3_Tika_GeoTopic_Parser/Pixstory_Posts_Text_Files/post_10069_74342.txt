In general the Afghan family is a model of unity and solidarity It operates as a unit economically in that the resources of the family are pooled both for the ordinary expenses and also for large capital outlays Marriage partners are often chosen within the extended family One s honor is intimately bound up with the name of the family In short the family provides its members with food shelter employment and security from cradle to coffin The Afghan family is usually emotionally and physically much closer than its western counterpart The physical closeness due to limited living space available for most families is complemented by an emotional closeness and interdependency The individual finds most of their social life within the limits of the extended family The family room provides the centre for story telling family discussions etc The individual who would want to go off and be alone would not be typical and if persistent would be viewed suspiciou
