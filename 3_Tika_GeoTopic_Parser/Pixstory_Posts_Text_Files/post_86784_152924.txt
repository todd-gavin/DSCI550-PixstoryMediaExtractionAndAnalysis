Qatar on Wednesday announced that the number of government workers attending offices would be slashed and school hours reduced in a bid to ease congestion before and during the World Cup With more than one million visitors expected during the November to December tournament the government is anxious to keep traffic off the streets around the eight stadiums Authorities are most worried about the first two weeks of the event when the peak number of foreign fans will be following the participating nations An official spokesperson said after a cabinet meeting that only percent of government staff would be expected in offices from November until December and staff would only work four hours a day From November to November school hours will be reduced opening only from am to midday and students will then be on holiday from November until December The main Corniche highway along Doha s seafront will be closed to all cars from November
