Societal pressures to maintain an ideal body weight lead many people to attempt to reduce or control their caloric intake through diets involving hedonic deprivation.

Despite earnest efforts, dieters seldom experience long-term success at weight loss, and restrained eating has ironically been linked with overeating and other disordered eating behavior.

“Hedonic deprivation” is the restriction of highly palatable foods that an individual enjoys eating.

 Mindful eating is posed as an alternative to hedonic deprivation that has shown efficacy at reducing overeating behaviors and managing weight in a sustainable way without the negative effects commonly associated with weight-loss dieting
