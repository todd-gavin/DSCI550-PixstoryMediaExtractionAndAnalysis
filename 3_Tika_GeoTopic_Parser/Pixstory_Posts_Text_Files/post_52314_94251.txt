Once upon a time more than a century ago in the small town of Cootamundra New South Wales Donald George was born the man who set such high batting standards in his time that the phrase second only to The Don has become cricket jargon Sir Don Bradman surpassed the pinnacle of batsmanship and then some in Test matches spanning years and a World War to an extent that is considered insurmountable to this day In Australian cricket he was often regarded as a god and he went on to form the Invincibles team In contrast to the asymptotic greats The Don was so far ahead of the average batsman that no player has come within an arm s length of his Test average points and nine fours in more than a century
