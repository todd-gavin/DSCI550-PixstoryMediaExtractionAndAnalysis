Early in his career, NBA icon Michael Jordan wore a pair of sneakers that sold for about $1.5 million on Sunday, establishing a record for game-used footwear at auction, according to Sotheby's. In the fifth game of his rookie season with the Chicago Bulls, the legendary player wore white leather shoes with a red Nike swoosh and soles. At the time, Nike's Jordan-affiliated brand was just beginning to gain popularity as an icon both on and off the court.

Michael Jordan's 1984 Nike Air Ships from the regular season, which were the most expensive sneakers ever offered at auction, just fetched $1,472,000 in our luxury sale in Las Vegas, the auction house announced on Twitter.
