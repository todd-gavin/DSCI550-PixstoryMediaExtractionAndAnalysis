By NewsDrum Desk New Delhi Polling to elect the next Vice President of India began at am with Prime Minister Narendra Modi being among the first ones to cast vote The polling will continue till pm following which the counting of ballots will take place National Democratic Alliance NDA nominee and former West Bengal governor Jagdeep Dhankhar is pitted against joint Opposition candidate Margaret Alva With the ruling BJP having an absolute majority in the Lok Sabha and members in the Rajya Sabha Dhankhar has a clear edge over his rival He is likely to succeed incumbent M Venkaiah Naidu whose tenure ends on August All MPs of the Lok Sabha and the Rajya Sabha including nominated members are entitled to vote in the vice presidential election The two Houses of Parliament together have a sanctioned strength of MPs of which there are eight vacancies in the Upper House Hence MPs are eligible to cast their ballot in this election
