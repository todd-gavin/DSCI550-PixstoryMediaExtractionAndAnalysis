The liberal state was established between and lasted until the years following the First World War The fundamental characteristics are as follows sovereignty belongs to the people representative principle system of political representation based on elections principle of legality the functioning and organization of the state are governed by law separation of powers Parliaments are introduced and powers are separated Parliament has the task of adopting laws The King has the task of enforcing them while the judges possess the power to verify their application and to sanction transgressors minimum state principle of individual liberty citizens become holders of subjective positions that can be protected before the judges inviolable rights and recognition of a private and individual sphere free from external interference
