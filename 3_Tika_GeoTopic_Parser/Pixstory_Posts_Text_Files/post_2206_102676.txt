Telangana Rashtra Samithi TRS supremo and Telangana chief minister K Chandrashekhar Rao or KCR as he is popularly known continues to be on his mission to forge a national alternative On Thursday he flew to Bengaluru to meet former Prime Minister HD Deve Gowda and his son HD Kumaraswamy to discuss the possibility of having a non BJP and non Congress alternative in the country Gowda s Janata Dal United ruled Karnataka from to at the head of a coalition government that also had Congress as it s constituent KCR went to Bengaluru on the day when Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrived in Hyderabad to address students of the Indian School of Business ISB on its th anniversary KCR had earlier met Delhi chief minister and Aam Aadmi Party AAP supremo Arvind Kejriwal and even travelled with him to Punjab The two leaders visited a school in Delhi and KCR later distributed cheques among the kin of farmers who died during the year long agitation over farm laws KCR also discussed the possibility of an opposition front with Samajwadi Party SP president and former Uttar Pradesh chief minister Akhilesh Yadav KCR also shares an excellent rapport with West Bengal chief minister and Trinamool Congress supremo Mamata Banerjee as well as Tamil Nadu chief minister and Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam DMK president MK Stalin With these meetings KCR is clearly trying to isolate the Congress among the opposition grouping The Nationalist Congress Party NCP and its boss Sharad Pawar are wary of such an initiative So is Jharkhand Mukti Morcha JMM and Jharkhand chief minister Hemant Soren and the Shiv Sena According to them the Congress has a pan India presence and hence can be a fulcrum for any opposition grouping The problem for KCR is that he cannot have any truck with the Congress given that his TRS and the grand old party are political arch rivals in Telangana KCR s plan is to ensure that the regional parties bag maximum Lok Sabha seats in their states to be able to dictate terms at the national level Once the negotiations begin he can also bargain for the Prime Minister s post Watch out for him
