Despite being dubbed the "great novelist of our generation," Richard Flanagan's breakthrough came with the publication of The Narrow Road to the Deep North in 2013. The novel, which won the Man Booker Prize in 2014, explores the story of prisoners of war held in Japanese labour camps along the Thai-Burma railway. 

Flanagan's stories are set in the context of Australia's rich history, with his forefathers arriving as convicts. He has brought literary acclaim to the little state of Tasmania on his own.

The novel is a novel about war's harshness, the fragility of life, and the impossibility of love.
