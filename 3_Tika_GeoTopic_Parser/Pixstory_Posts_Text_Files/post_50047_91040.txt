The romanticism that focuses on irrational devilish or grotesque Edgar Allan Poe is one of the prominent figures of Dark Romanticism Dark Romanticism introduces the levels of the crooked atmosphere which could include ghosts ghouls etc The name Dark Romanticism came from the literary theorist Mario Praz in his lengthy study of the genre published in The Romantic Agony These could also include forms of Satan devils ghosts werewolves vampires and ghouls as emblematic of human nature according to the critic G R Thompson This genre focus on the gullibility of mankind into sin self destruction which is exactly the opposite of traditional romanticism Authors who embrace this genre include Edgar Allan Poe Nathaniel Hawthorne Herman Melville and Emily Dickinson Examples include The Scarlet Letter by Nathaniel Hawthorne The Fall of the House of Usher by Edgar Allen Poe
