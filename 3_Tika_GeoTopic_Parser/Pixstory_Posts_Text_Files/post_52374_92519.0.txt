Track and field is the most popular sport among girls, with 488,267 participants. It is the most popular sport for girls in 16 states. While volleyball, the second most popular sport for girls, has fewer participants, it is the most popular sport in 21 states across the country. According to our Aspley research, netball, football, and basketball are the top three team club sports for female participation. Gymnastics and swimming are also popular club sport activities for younger girls, while women prefer tennis and Australian football. Gymnastics, dance, and figure skating are common examples of sports appropriate for female participation, owing to their expressive qualities and graceful movements designed to visually please the audience by emphasising the athletes' feminine physiques.
