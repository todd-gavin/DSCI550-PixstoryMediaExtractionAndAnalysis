The official end of ovulation is called menopause Just like there is a natural age when a woman starts to ovulate there comes a time when she stops ovulating and will not get pregnant anymore One is officially diagnosed with menopause when she does not get her periods for months continuously On average a woman hits menopause between the ages of late s to early s However ovulation can stop before this month phase as well or can happen very irregularly for a few months leading up to these months There is a period of time before officially hitting menopause called perimenopause During this stage it is possible for a woman to be pregnant since she may still ovulate but not frequently The classic symptoms which indicate that a woman is perimenopausal are irregular menstrual cycle skipping periods thinning hair skin drying up hot flashes sleep problems mood swings weight gain vaginal dryness decreased metabolism etc Age range and factors determine when you actually get menopause A woman is born with all the approx million eggs These eggs are initially in the form of egg cells until a woman hits puberty Every month on average eggs die before a woman hits puberty The egg death and degeneration continues even for mature eggs at an accelerated pace as we age By the time a woman enters her late s especially by age there is a significant decline in reproductive hormones that regulate menstruation This eventually reduces the number of eggs produced resulting in a decline in fertility Almost by the age of or later a woman starts experiencing irregular or skipped periods varying period flow etc People at higher risk of early menopause include those who have had oophorectomy surgery to remove ovaries hysterectomy uterus removal surgery chemotherapy radiation therapy or have a history of early menopause in bloodline Sources
