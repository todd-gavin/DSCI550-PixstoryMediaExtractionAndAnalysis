Beginning at 7:15 a.m. EDT on Wednesday, Sept. 21, NASA will broadcast live commentary of the upcoming Artemis I cryogenic demonstration test. Teams will be able to assess improved propellant loading processes, check the correction of a hydrogen leak that was discovered during an Artemis I launch attempt, and do additional evaluations during the demonstration test. When the test's goals are accomplished, the demonstration will come to an end. On NASA Television, the NASA app, and the agency's website, there will be live coverage of the test. The Artemis I demonstration test will air concurrently with NASA's coverage of the launch, rendezvous, docking, and hatch opening of the Soyuz MS-22 delivering NASA Astronaut Frank Rubio to the International Space Station on NASA's Television's Public Channel.the Artemis I demonstration test will air only on the Media Channel. During all other times, the test will air on both the Public and Media Channels.The agency also will host a media teleconference to preview the test at 11:30 a.m. Monday, Sept. 19. Source: NASA TV
