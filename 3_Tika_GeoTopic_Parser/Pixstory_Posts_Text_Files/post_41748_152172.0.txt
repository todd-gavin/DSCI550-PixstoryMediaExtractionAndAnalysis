Cricket Australia (CA) apologised to those who had survived child sex abuse in the nation's sport on Monday and stated that the Board is looking into methods to assist victims. As per IE, in a statement, CA Chair Dr. Lachlan Henderson called historical sexual abuse a "appalling issue." Former Australia Under-19 cricketer Jamie Mitchell claimed in the beginning of this year that squad members "sexually molested" him in 1985 while the team was on tour in Sri Lanka.The cricketing body is also set to discuss the issue during its next board meeting.“We can’t change what happened but we need to do what we can to assist victims. On behalf of CA I want to apologise to anyone who has suffered sexual abuse while involved in Australian Cricket,” Henderson said in a statement.
