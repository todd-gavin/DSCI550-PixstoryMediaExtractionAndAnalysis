Croissant A croissant is a French bread It has a buttery, flaky texture It names us after its crescent shape The unique feature of this world famous bread is that it was made using the technique called lamination Wailing involves the dough covered in butter rolled and folded several times then rolled into a thin sheet This is a typical breakfast bread The dough contains yeast which makes the bread soft The croissants will keep for one or more two days at room temperature The croissant originates from the Austrian kipfel but later became French August Zang was the first French baker to invest in a croissant at the beginning of the 17th century
