For nine days in May Lakshmi Devi toiled under a broiling sun hoping to earn Rs per day joining others like her desperate for work in digging a pond a job offered to them as an economic safety net under India s national jobs for work programme Lakshmi Devi needed the job and money and so she enrolled under the scheme and currently walks kms daily to reach her work site at am working for the next hours Since the pandemic struck in her husband a private construction worker who earned Rs per day had been in and out of work The money would help her run her household and care for her two year old daughter She needs milk everyday and I need oil to cook it has become so expensive now she said In her village of Kukar Khera in the southern Rajasthan district of Rajsamand over km south of the capital Jaipur she worked under the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act between and May A muster roll which is essentially a list of names of workers at a specific site put together to mark their attendance was prepared and Lakshmi Devi s name was included in it When pay day came Lakshmi Devi was shocked she was paid Rs instead of Rs payment for four days of work not nine Distraught she approached the mate or the site supervisor who registered the attendance of the workers on the National Mobile Monitoring System App using her smartphone It is compulsory for mates across the country to register the attendance of crore active NREGA workers through the app a new feature introduced by the Centre in May this year I asked her why she cut my attendance but she showed me the physical muster roll where she marked it said Lakshmi Devi Read More
