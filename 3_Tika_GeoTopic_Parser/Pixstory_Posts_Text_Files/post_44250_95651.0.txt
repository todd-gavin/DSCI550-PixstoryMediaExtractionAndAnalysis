Lionel Messi, a product of FC Barcelona's development system, was promoted to the senior team during the 2004-05 season and made his La Liga debut against local rivals Espanyol on October 16, 2004.

In the 82nd minute of the encounter, the Argentine came on as a substitute to replace Deco. Messi became the youngest player to represent Barcelona in an official competition at the age of 17 years and three months. Despite the fact that Messi had played for Barcelona's senior team against FC Porto a year previously, it was a friendly match between the two teams and not an official match.
 
Let's take a look at the players that were on the field with Messi when he made his La Liga debut for Barcelona, and where they are now.
 
Henrik Larsson – FW

From 2004 to 2006, the former Sweden international striker spent two seasons with Barcelona. He also played for Feyenoord, Celtic, and Manchester United in addition to Barcelona. He announced his retirement from professional football in 2009, however he returned in 2012 and 2013 to play a match for Swedish clubs Raa IF and Hogaborgs BK, respectively.

He has served as a head coach and assistant coach for a number of Swedish clubs, including Landskrona BoIS, Hogaborgs, and Falkenbergs FF, since 2010. Larsson joined Ronaldo Koeman's coaching staff after he became head coach of Barcelona in 2020, and he is presently working as an assistant coach for the Spanish club.

Samuel Eto'o – RW

From 2004 until 2009, the great Cameroon striker spent five seasons with Barcelona. Inter Milan, Chelsea, and Everton were among his latter clubs. After a season with Qatar SC, he retired from professional football in 2019. He is presently the Qatar World Cup 2022 Legacy Ambassador.

Ronaldinho – LW

From 2003 through 2008, the Brazilian great performed in Barcelona. He won six titles during that time, including two La Ligas and one Champions League. Ronaldinho announced his retirement from professional football in January 2018.
