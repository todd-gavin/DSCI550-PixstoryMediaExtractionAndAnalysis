A state-of-the-art artificial retina restores the vision of light to a blind seventy-year-old affected by retinitis pigmentosa The operation which lasted about hours was carried out at the Gemelli Irccs Polyclinic in Rome by professor Stanislao Rizzo director of the Ophthalmological Unit Upon awakening the patient suffering from a severe form of retinitis pigmentosa that had caused the loss of vision was already able to perceive light The new retina equipped with more electrodes is currently indicated only for patients suffering from retinitis pigmentosa in the more advanced stages, i.e. those who have completely lost sight on both sides eyes Read more
