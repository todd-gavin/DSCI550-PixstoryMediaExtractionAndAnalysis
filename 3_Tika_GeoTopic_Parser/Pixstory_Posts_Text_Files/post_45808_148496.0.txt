On Thursday, September 29, three cosmonauts who now reside on the International Space Station are scheduled to come down to Earth. On September 29, at 3:34 a.m. EDT, Expedition 67 Commander Oleg Artemyev of Roscosmos and fellow cosmonauts Denis Matveev and Sergey Korsakov will undock from the station's Prichal module in the Soyuz MS-21 spacecraft. More than four hours later, at 6:57 a.m. EDT, the group will depart for a parachute-assisted landing on the steppe of Kazakhstan southeast of the isolated town of Dzhezkazgan (4:57 p.m. Kazakhstan time). Live coverage of the crew's change of command, undocking, and landing will be available at the following times (all Eastern) on NASA Television, the agency website, and the NASA app!The three cosmonauts launched to the station on March 18, and are completing a mission of 185 days and 2,960 orbits of the Earth spanning 77 million miles.Source: NASA TV
