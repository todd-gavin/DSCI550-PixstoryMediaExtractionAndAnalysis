Rachel Carson was well known in America for her passion for the environment Originally a marine biologist and conservationist she is attributed for advancing the global environmental movement She was a writer who wrote to bring out a change mostly in environmental issues Her book Although Silent Spring met with fierce opposition from chemical companies it sparked a reversal in national pesticide policy which led to a nationwide ban on DDT and other pesticides It also provoked a grassroots environmental movement that led to the creation of the U S Environmental Protection Agency She was concerned about the use of synthetic pesticides many of which had been formulated through the military funding of science since World War II However the United States federal government s gypsy moth now called spongy moth eradication program urged Carson to dedicate her research and her next book to pesticides and environmental poisons The gypsy moth program involved aerial spraying of DDT and other pesticides mixed with fuel oil including the spraying of private land Landowners on Long Island filed a lawsuit to have the spraying stopped and many in affected regions followed the case closely Though the suit was lost the Supreme Court granted petitioners the right to gain ultimata against potential environmental devastation in the future this laid the basis for later successful environmental action Carson was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Jimmy Carter
