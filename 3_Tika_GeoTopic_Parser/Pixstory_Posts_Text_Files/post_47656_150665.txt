By Shailesh Khanduri Mumbai The Reserve Bank of India RBI on Friday raised the benchmark lending rate by basis points to per cent in a bid to check inflation which has remained above its tolerance level for the past months With the latest hike the repo rate or the short term lending rate at which banks borrow from the central bank is now close to per cent This is the fourth consecutive rate hike after a basis points increase in May and basis points hike each in June and August In all RBI has raised benchmark rate by per cent since May this year The six member Monetary Policy Committee MPC headed by RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das decided in favour of the rate hike The Consumer Price Index CPI based inflation which RBI factors in while fixing its benchmark rate stood at per cent in August Retail inflation has been ruling above the RBI s comfort level of per cent since January this year Das retained the inflation projection at per cent for the current fiscal while slashing real GDP growth estimate to per cent from earlier forecast of per cent for FY The latest RBI action follows the US Federal Reserve effecting the third consecutive percentage point interest rate increase taking its benchmark rate to a range of per cent earlier this month
