While there are some people who believe that potatoes are bad for your health, the facts show that they offer plenty of nutrients that are good for you. In particular, potatoes are a great source of potassium, vitamin C, vitamin B6, and fiber. They're also a good source of copper and manganese.

Potatoes can help to reduce the risk of heart disease, stroke, Type 2 diabetes, and certain types of cancer. They can also help to regulate blood sugar levels and improve cholesterol levels. In addition, potatoes contain antioxidants which protect cells from damage and help to prevent diseases like Alzheimer's from developing.
