India has strongly refuted the World Health Organisation's use of a statistically unsound method of data collection to arrive at an exaggerated estimate for the number of deaths due to Covid in the country. 

"India has consistently questioned WHO's own admission that data in respect of 17 Indian states was obtained from some websites and media reports and was used in their mathematical model," the Union Health Ministry said in a statement.

"This reflects a statistically unsound and scientifically questionable methodology of data collection for making excess mortality projections in case of India," the statement added.
