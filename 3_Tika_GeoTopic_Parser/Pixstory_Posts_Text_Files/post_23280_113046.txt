As of July, same-sex couples can get married in Switzerland, as is already the case in most Western European countries. Associations defending the rights of gay people have been demanding the right to civil marriage in Switzerland for nearly four decades. Their demands for have clashed with ultra-conservative circles In September, the Swiss people finally accepted via referendum a modification of the civil code that legalized same-sex marriages A significant further step forward from the other referendum that made homophobia a crime In Switzerland, in fact, despite the decriminalization of homosexuality in the police continued to keep gay records as possible proof of immorality in the event of crimes Attempts to block marriage for all by the most conservative wing of the country have failed in the face of a large referendum majority which brings Switzerland to the forefront of the advocacy of LGBTIQ rights
