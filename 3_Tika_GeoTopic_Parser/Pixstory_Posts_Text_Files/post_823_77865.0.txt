Using three shipping containers for its structure and recycled wood pallets as a facade, 85% of the materials that make up the Infiniski Manifesto House -- located in Curacaví, Chile, and completed in 2009 -- are recycled.

 Architect Jaime Gaztelu integrated energy-saving technology in the design, reducing consumption by 70% through renewable water and space heating, and using cellulose extracted from unread newspapers as insulation.
