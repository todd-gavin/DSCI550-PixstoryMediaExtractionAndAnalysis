Harlem Renaissance was the name given to a period of creativity in literature music dance art by African Americans from s to s Named after The New Negro a anthology edited by Alain Locke it was known as the New Negro Movement The movement also comprised the new African American cultural manifestations across the urban areas in the Northeast and Midwest United States affected by a renewed militancy in the general struggle for civil rights combined with the Great Migration of African American workers fleeing the racist conditions of the Jim Crow Deep South They mainly consist of stories poetry fictions by African American writers During the Harlem Renaissance movement Black writers created work that celebrated Black culture and folklore Black poets authors and essayists wrote thousands of pieces that helped lay the framework for the civil rights movement to follow decades later Notable Harlem Renaissance Writers and Poets includes Claude McKay Home to Harlem James Weldon Johnson Lift Every Voice and Sing Langston Hughes The Weary Blues
